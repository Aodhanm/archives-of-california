Document

Gov. Luis Antonio Argüello to Prefect Fr. Vicente Francisco de Sarría, Monterey, 17 September 1823 — the governor's answer to the prefect's opposition to the Mission San Francisco transfer to Sonoma. The clearest statement in the archive that the mission system had become, for the civil government, a territorial instrument. This letter spans catalog records ca46-d43 and ca46-d44.

Key passages (transcription)

…la Mis.n de S.n Fran.co empezó á desmerecer p.r haberse segregado bast.e de sus Neofitos…

[Citing the Constitution, Art. 335 §10: the Diputación's authority over] "la economía, órd.n y progresos de las Misiones y la conversión de los Ind.s infieles."

…su escolta p.a precaverla de invasiones…

después de 50 y tantos años de fundada esta prov.a, diga V.R. q.e progresos ha tenido en esta parte?

…adelantar, ó propiamente progresar… [quien intente] á la sordina estorbar este adelantamiento en la extensión de la prov.a… ni me hará retroceder ning.a oposición…

Translation

…the Mission of San Francisco began to decline because so many of its neophytes had been separated from it… [the Diputación's constitutional authority over] "the economy, order, and progress of the Missions and the conversion of the infidel Indians"… its escort, to guard it against invasions… After fifty-some years since this province was founded, tell me, Your Reverence, what progress has been made in this regard? …to advance, or properly to progress… [whoever attempts] quietly to obstruct this advancement in the extension of the province [will not succeed]… nor will any opposition make me retreat…

Editorial note

Argüello argues from political sovereignty (the Mexican Constitution), not ecclesiastical tradition; the escort is justified against "invasions" — the Russian establishment at Ross/Bodega being the invasion in view.
