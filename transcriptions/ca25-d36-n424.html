Key passages with translation · vault working file (sola-to-viceroy-april-1818) · C-A 25, Prov. Rec. Tomo IX, orig. pp. 583–604 (Savage pp. 176–196, scans n424–n444)

Document

Gov. Pablo Vicente de Solá to the Viceroy, Monterey, 3 April 1818 — the ~21-page frontier report written fifteen months after San Rafael's founding: the Russian establishments, the reconnaissances, and the state of the province.

Key passages (transcription)

Que [la] Mision de Sn Francisco, desde su ingreso á la Provincia le ha llamado la atencion por el gran n.º de muertos que anualmente se experimenta… [San Rafael founded] con la mira tambien de atraer los muchos gentiles…

Que la nueva Cal. en la Costa Setentrional del Mar Pacifico se estiende desde Sn Diego hasta Sn Francisco, último punto del Norte…

Que las frecuentes cruzadas y arribadas á la Costa por Buques extrangeros, Anglo Americanos, Ingleses y Rusos en solicitud de víveres… Que se le manden auxilios que tiene pedidos…

Translation

That the Mission of San Francisco, since his arrival in the Province, has drawn his attention for the great number of deaths experienced annually… [San Rafael was founded] with the aim also of attracting the many gentiles… That New California on the northern coast of the Pacific Sea extends from San Diego to San Francisco, the last point of the North… That the frequent crossings and arrivals on the coast by foreign vessels — Anglo-American, English, and Russian — in search of provisions… [he asks] that the aid he has requested be sent…

Editorial note

The "último punto del Norte" formulation independently echoes the earlier administrative report's "último punto que podemos poseer con completo é independiente dominio" (C-A 54, Doc IV-26) — two officials, two decades apart, naming San Francisco the limit. The report also carries the royal order to "dislodge the Russians from Bodega" with Solá's plea for reinforcements (Savage p. 196). ⚠ Bancroft's page reference for this report ("Prov. Rec., MS., ix. 777") does not match Savage's numbering — unresolved.
