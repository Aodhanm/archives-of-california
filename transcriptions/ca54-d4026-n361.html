Document

Undated administrative report (late 1790s–1804 by internal evidence) on California's fiscal system, recommending the division of the Californias. The passage below fixes the northern limit of secure Spanish sovereignty.

Transcription (the key sentence)

[Quedando á la Nueva California] el presidio de San Diego hasta el de San Francisco inclusive, como último punto que podemos poseer con completo é independiente dominio en la costa del Sur, según el convenio hecho con la Corte de Londres en el año de 90 [digit uncertain: 90/93]; señalándose los límites en la misión de San Miguel y el presidio de San Diego…

Translation

[New California to keep] from the presidio of San Diego to that of San Francisco inclusive — as the last point we can possess with complete and independent dominion on the [Pacific] coast, according to the agreement made with the Court of London in the year [17]90 [the Nootka Convention]…

Editorial note

A Spanish administrative report stating, in its own words, that San Francisco is the northernmost point Spain can hold "with complete and independent dominion," and tying that limit to the Nootka settlement with Britain. Cautions before citing: the year digit (90 vs. 93) is uncertain; the report is undated and its authorship unestablished; the phrase "según" should not be pressed into a formal treaty citation until the report's provenance is fixed.
