E4: The centralist extinction (1839) and constitutional restoration (1844) of the Los Angeles ayuntamiento

Scans: n97 · n109–n120 · n365–n366 | Orig pp. V·97, V·109–120, VIII·1–2 | Type: abstracts of decrees + bandos (Savage copy) | Docs: 301, 355 (1839) · 959, 960 (1844)

The volume brackets a full institutional cycle. Under the 1836 centralist régime the Los Angeles ayuntamiento is dissolved: Doc 301 (7 Nov 1839) reorganizes it so the alcaldes continue only as interim Jueces de Paz; Doc 355 ("Extinción del Ayuntamiento") makes it definitive — abolishing the ayuntamiento and the Síndico posts, transferring funds to a Prefecture-controlled Depositario, and extending the measure to all pueblos under the LA Prefecture. Five years later, Tomo VIII opens (1844) with the restored constitutional Ayuntamiento under Alcalde 1º Manuel Requena, immediately legislating — a 21-article ornato/policía bando (959) and a rural-celador roster mapping the basin's ranchos (960).

Significance: a clean primary illustration of how national constitutional swings (centralism 1836 → the 1843 Bases Orgánicas / restored municipal government 1844) reached down into the concrete machinery of Los Angeles self-government — the abolition and re-creation of the town's own corporation, its treasury, and its policing.
