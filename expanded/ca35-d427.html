E5: The Graham Affair aftermath — the deportees, the President's disposition, and the return (1841)

Scans: n149–n160 · n165–n167 | Orig pp. VI·? (doc pp.—) | Type: abstracts of gubernatorial/court correspondence (Savage copy) | Docs: 427, 443, 439 (+ 457's published circular)

The Los Angeles district's paper trail of the 1840 Isaac Graham affair as it played out in 1841: the President's disposition on the foreign prisoners — Graham, Alberto Morris, Guillermo Chard, and Jorge (George) held abroad, the naturalized / Mexican-married spared, the rest expelled and barred from returning to California (Doc 427); Capt. José Castro conducting the "presa" [the deportees] to the Republic's capital (443); and the Graham-Affair circular published at San Diego as part of the frontier arming (in 457). Alongside runs the local prosecution of Eugenia Valencia's complicity in the Fink murder and the condemned Santiago Linares's chapel plea for forgiveness (439), which the register interleaves with the Graham material.

Significance: a southern-district vantage on a famously northern episode — showing the Graham deportation as a departmental administrative operation (custody, transport, publication of terms) and its entanglement with the frontier-defense mobilization and local criminal justice.
