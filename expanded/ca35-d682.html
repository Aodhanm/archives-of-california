E7: Governor Micheltorena in the south — arrival, the drained ex-mission economy, and the 1844 fall (1842–1845)

Scans: n252 · n265–n292 · n398–n406 | Orig pp. VI·129, VII·7–34, VIII·34–42 | Type: running abstract of provisioning receipts, bandos & political correspondence (Savage copy) | Docs: 682, 715–729, 741–763, 765, 767, 777, 796, 803, 806 (1842–43) · 1034, 1046 (1844 commissions) · 1051, 1054 (Nov-1844 pronunciamiento) · 1062, 1072, 1078 (post-revolt militia, 1845)

The volume gives the southern-district experience of the Micheltorena governorship from first rumor to overthrow:
- Advance word: a schooner from Guaymas reports "un general con 500 hombres" coming (682, 3 July 1842 — later confirmed as Micheltorena).
- Provisioning (Sept–Nov 1842): the whole 2º-district is mobilized to feed his battalion's overland march to Monterey — "se está preparando todo para la expedición y la Casa de Sánchez es la más decente para recibir a Micheltorena" (715); the 16-Sept fiesta postponed until he arrives (716); a near-continuous stream of receipts (horses, cattle, mules, carts, flour, firewood, and **Indian molenderos from San Gabriel, 717–763), with repeated pleas of exhaustion — La Purísima "no puede… por no tener nada" (720) and Carrillo's report that the ex-missions, "a pesar de su decadencia," can still yield "importantes recursos" (777).
- Arrival & installation: he reaches Los Angeles ~5 Nov 1842 (765) with a hospital sought for his sick soldiers (767); Jimeno hands him the political command (806) and he is formally installed governor with a 15-gun salute (803).
- The fall (Nov 1844): the pronunciamiento against him reaches LA — the 1 Nov northern pronouncement (1051) — and Pío Pico, as 1st vocal of the Assembly, positions himself constitutionally (1054). Foreshadowed by Micheltorena's own commissions of Pío Pico as Comandante de Escuadrón (1034) and Palomares as captain (1046); followed by the post-revolt militia reorganization — the March-1845 horse-mobilization (1062), the muster of the Permanent Company (1072), and Andrés Pico taking military command of the plaza (1078).

Significance: a concrete, ledger-level demonstration of how Micheltorena's regime was seated on — and helped exhaust — the already-collapsing ex-mission economy of the south**, and how quickly californio elites turned the constitutional machinery (the Assembly, the militia commissions he himself granted) against him.
