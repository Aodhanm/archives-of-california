# The William Glen Rae suicide investigation — the death of D. Guillermo (William) Glen Rae, HBC agent at Yerba Buena; wit

**C-A 39 (Departmental State Papers, Benicia), scan n449–n453** · Sav p. 449–453 · tomo p. 1155–1206 · 1845-01 · verbatim transcription · 

The William Glen Rae suicide investigation — the death of D. Guillermo (William) Glen Rae, HBC agent at Yerba Buena; witnesses Nathan Spear, Jas. C. Davis, John Ross, Wm. Reynolds, Nathan Spear, Concepción Ávila (Mrs. Fuller), Mrs. Fuller, Sinclair, Hinckley describe Rae's melancholy, the pistol, the botella of poison (opium tincture); ⭐⭐⭐ Vice-Consul Forbes confirms suicide & remits Rae's last document (Alta Cal., Bahía de San Francisco, Yerba Buena, 20 Jan 1845) transferring all HBC 'Bahía de Hudson' goods & establishment to Forbes & to Jacobo P. Leese as sobrecargo, 'á beneficio de… mi esposa Dña Eliza McLoughlin Rae'; the real raíces of the HBC to remain with Forbes until Dn Juan McLoughlin or the HBC's Columbia agent arrives; Judge declared the suicide proven & drew testimonio for the Superioridad (ends orig p.1206)
