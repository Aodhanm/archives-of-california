# José Salazar's Indian expedition & skirmish — a partida de indios gentiles at a rancho disarmed (20 arcos, 20 carcajes);

**C-A 39 (Departmental State Papers, Benicia), scan n430–n435** · Sav p. 430–435 · tomo p. 34–39 · 1841[?] · verbatim transcription · 

José Salazar's Indian expedition & skirmish — a partida de indios gentiles at a rancho disarmed (20 arcos, 20 carcajes); Salazar ordered to conduct the Indians to the capital; they resisted with mazas & cuchillos, came upon the 12-man force; forced to fire in self-defense — 18 indios killed, 1 taken prisoner, 2 wounded on Salazar's side ('uno se fué bueno y 2 heridos')
