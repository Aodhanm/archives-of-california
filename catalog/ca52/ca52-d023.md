# narrative of battles with armed NW-Coast Indians — req. permission to repair his frigate; no water ('Indios tan bárbaros

**C-A 52 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n88–94** · Sav p.  · tomo p. 89–95 · 1793/94-08-12 · Savage abstract · 

narrative of battles with armed NW-Coast Indians — req. permission to repair his frigate; no water ('Indios tan bárbaros'); 1st battle at 'Pto de Tequin,' Chatham Strait (300 attackers w/ 2–3 muskets each, pistols, daggers; 3 assaults/day repelled); a cove w/ ~1,000 Indians; the chief 'Piecque' ('hombre malo') summoned all Indians 'desde la Estrecha de Juan de Fuca hasta la punta del Norte de Nutka' in 3 days to seize the ship + ceremonial dances

> [!note] Re-dated 2026-07-14 (ship-registry adjudication): the Savage header reads [1]803 (leaf read at scale 2, scans n84/n88). The old 1793/94 reading is impossible — Brown's *Alexander* first reached the coast in 1803 (cf. ca11-d268) and Bancroft's first-US-ship = the *Otter*, 1796. The '93-vs-1794 survey flag is hereby resolved: both docs = August 1803; Arrillaga (gov. 1803) also being interim gov. 1792–94 is how the misreading survived.
