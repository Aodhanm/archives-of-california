# Comprehensive report on the Commerce & Revenues of California (see Entry 10): the situado's cessation since 1810; Solá's

**C-A 28 (Departmental State Papers), scan n192–n205** · Sav p. 191–204 · tomo p. iii.762–780 · 1834-11-28 · verbatim transcription · 

Comprehensive report on the Commerce & Revenues of California (see Entry 10): the situado's cessation since 1810; Solá's ~1819 opening of the ports to foreign trade (the growing Sandwich Islands trade); the 1827 Maritime Tariff; the 1829 Padrés customs reform; Echeandía's port practices; Monterey as the sole port of entry; the geography forcing a coasting trade; California's economic dependence on foreign trade (enforcing prohibitions would breed 'desafecto al gobierno y motivos de subversión'); shortages of rice/sugar/tobacco/gunpowder; staffing proposals (Admin. Ángel Ramírez at $2,000); concludes with a 4-point legislative proposal: (1) habilitate Monterey for foreign commerce, (2) habilitate SF/Sta Bárbara/San Pedro/San Diego for cabotage, (3) permit foreign coasting trade 5 more years, (4) establish a Monterey Customs House ⚠ UNCITED (Sav pp.191-204)
