# Figueroa's political indictment of the Missionary Padres (per the supreme order of 17 May 1833, weighing Echeandía's acc

**C-A 28 (Departmental State Papers), scan n140–n141** · Sav p. 139–140 · tomo p. iii.551–554 · 1834-08-17 · verbatim transcription · 

Figueroa's political indictment of the Missionary Padres (per the supreme order of 17 May 1833, weighing Echeandía's accusation vs Victoria's defense of Sarría & Durán): their private/religious conduct is 'ejemplar,' BUT their political conduct is 'no está de acuerdo con los intereses nacionales' — as Prelados they spread their politics through their Colegio; the missionaries publicly refused to swear the Federal Constitution, some reject independence, don't celebrate national fiestas, oppose the repartimiento de tierras (secularization), freedom of the press & popular sovereignty, and advocate restoring the Inquisition; their ascendancy + wealth make them 'hombres peligrosos en el caso de una invasión'; concludes recommending the expulsion of Sarría, Durán & all like them. The ideological core of the secularization campaign (cf. Docs 85, 95) ⚠ UNCITED gem (Sav p.140)
