# Report on the Federal Offices & Employees of California — a full history of the fiscal administration 1825–1834: before 

**C-A 28 (Departmental State Papers), scan n210–n212** · Sav p. 209–211 · tomo p. iii.796–805 · 1834-12-06 · Savage abstract · 

Report on the Federal Offices & Employees of California — a full history of the fiscal administration 1825–1834: before 1825 the Presidial Habilitados managed public funds; 1825 Habilitados = Santiago Argüello (SD), Joaquín Maytorena (SB), José Mariano Estrada (Monterey), Ignacio Martínez (SF); Aug 1825 José María Herrera = Comisario Sub-Principal (resigned 1827); then Domingo Carrillo; Estrada → M. G. Vallejo; Manuel Jimeno Casarín (Dec 1828–Nov 1829, deposed by the Solís revolt), Osio briefly, Casarín again 1830, Joaquín Gómez 1831–32, Bandini Comisario Subalterno→Principal — recurring 'no ha rendido cuentas'; on arriving (Jan 1833) Figueroa found only Estrada, gave the Customs House to Rafael González, later Herrera (Oct 1834), and Ángel Ramírez to the Maritime Customs House (1 Jul 1834).
