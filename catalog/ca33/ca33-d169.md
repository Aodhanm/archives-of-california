# Sumaria against a foreign brig poaching sea otter (nutrias) off the California coast without license — an unknown foreig

**C-A 33 (Departmental State Papers (Juzgados / Naturalization)), scan n150–n156** · Sav p. 54–60 · tomo p. 332–338+ · 1838-06 → 1839-01 · Savage abstract · 

Sumaria against a foreign brig poaching sea otter (nutrias) off the California coast without license — an unknown foreign bergantín (~200 tons, 4–6 cañones, 20–25 crew) seen hunting nutrias at los Cayucos, otter skins aboard; Alvarado orders averiguaciones; sworn declarations gathered from Abel P. Stearns, [Henry] Fitch, Jorge (George) Nidever, Pedro Cárdenas, Esteban (Stephen) Simmons, Julián Carrillo, Francisco Ortega — the brig is named *Lima*, capitán Banewell [Bramwell/Bancroft?]; ⭐ 22 armed Indians (naturales de la costa del Brissel [Brazil?]) landed from it, hunting otter all along the coast, refusing to enter any port; Simmons saw six cannons mounted; a related note that in the otter-hunt some armed groups inflict harm on the cattle-owners (see Expanded Entry 6)
