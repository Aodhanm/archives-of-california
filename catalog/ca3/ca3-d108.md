# A SHIPWRECK on the Monterey-Bay coast (late 1784): at Santa Clara / the mouth of the Río Pájaro, 4–5 men killed, figures

**C-A 3 (Provincial State Papers), scan n104–n106** · Sav p. 105–106 · tomo p. v.210–212 · 1784-12-23 · Savage abstract · 

A SHIPWRECK on the Monterey-Bay coast (late 1784): at Santa Clara / the mouth of the Río Pájaro, 4–5 men killed, figures seen coming from the sea at night, a ship smashed on the rocks (per a Gentile); beach debris — sails, a boat-*palizada*, dead cattle, a buckled shoe (*zapato con hebillas*); Moraga proposes reconnoitering. Also notes the frigate *Princesa* (Capt. Esteban Martínez) left San Blas 12 Nov. ⚠ ship's identity/nationality unstated — investigate
