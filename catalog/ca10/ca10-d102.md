# BORICA'S INTERROGATORY OF THE AMERICAN SAILORS — the questions alférez Ma. Rodríguez will put to the American sailors [l

**C-A 10 (Provincial State Papers), scan n202–n206** · Sav p. 200–204 · tomo p. — · 1798-12-22 · Savage abstract · 

BORICA'S INTERROGATORY OF THE AMERICAN SAILORS — the questions alférez Ma. Rodríguez will put to the American sailors [left by the *Eliza*] 'y con particularidad al que entienda algún otro idioma': (1) how many English ships they've seen on the NW Coast ('la altura'), their ports, forces, captains' names & destinations; (2) at what points they conduct their *peletería* (fur) trade & how they trade with the Indians; (3) whether the English are settled ('de asiento') at Nootka or the Columbia… The full ~13-question set also probes the SANDWICH ISLANDS (Hawaii): foreign (English/American/Spanish) establishments there, European crops & livestock introduced, whether the native Hawaiian government has been subordinated to the foreigners, whether the English hold a fort/force there; and specifically the deserter *Francisco de Paula Marín* (the Spaniard who became Kamehameha's advisor) — with orders to cross-check the sailors' answers 'para sacar en limpio lo cierto.' A comprehensive 1798 Spanish intelligence sweep of the whole Pacific foreign presence (NW-Coast fur trade + Nootka/Columbia + Hawaii), tied to the *Eliza*'s crew. Ends n204, pp.434–35 (copy Borica 7 Feb 99)
