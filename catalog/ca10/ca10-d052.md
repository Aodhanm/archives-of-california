# THE FRIAR-WHISTLEBLOWER DOSSIER ON MISSION-INDIAN TREATMENT. Fr. Antonio de la Concepción (Fernandino, sent 1797 to foun

**C-A 10 (Provincial State Papers), scan n49–n69** · Sav p. 49–69+ / 90–… · 1798-07-12 (rep.); fwd. 31 Aug 1798 · Savage abstract · 

THE FRIAR-WHISTLEBLOWER DOSSIER ON MISSION-INDIAN TREATMENT. Fr. Antonio de la Concepción (Fernandino, sent 1797 to found Mission San Miguel) charges the P. Presidente Fr. Buenaventura Sitjar with being 'totalmente opuesto á cuanto está mandado por R. Cédulas… en orden á la crianza y educación de los indios': a dispute over teaching doctrine in the natives' own language (Sitjar) vs. Castilian (the cédulas); improper same-day adult baptisms; letting baptized Indians flee to the hills. Sitjar had him declared 'loco,' violently removed from the mission, and beaten on the road to Monterey (witnesses Fr. Antonio Peyri, Fr. Juan Manuel Fernández, Fr. Diego García, the presidio surgeon, & pilot Esteban Martínez). ⭐ Attached interrogatorio on mission conditions (numbered answers): neophyte housing (adobe/tile for married; single women & single men locked in dormitories at night by the padres); labor hours (winter 3+3, summer ~4, exempting pregnant/nursing/old/children); diet — *atole* morning, *pozole* midday & evening, 'que en el año no alcanza á 22 fanegas de grano por individuo' (deemed insufficient); clothing (a cotton blanket + 1–2 *frazadas*); punishment (the padres rarely consent, but the *gente de razón* & escort guards punish them, esp. at Mission San Francisco); language (missionaries 'indifferent' to Castilian); no Christian Indian to the hills 'sin licencia limitada'; the missions' cash/credits (San Carlos 862 p, Soledad 221, San Antonio 536+410, San Luis Obispo — 4 partidas = 1,966 p). A rare *insider* (friar) indictment of California mission discipline & material conditions — the 'Indian-cruelty charges' the completion plan flagged (the 15-question interrogatorio, answered by a first respondent, runs n60–n70)
