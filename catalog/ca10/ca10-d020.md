# Mission San Francisco's LAND-SHORTAGE representation (church-vs-state): citing the *Recopilación de Indias* (lib. 6 tít.

**C-A 10 (Provincial State Papers), scan n13–n16** · Sav p. 13–16 · tomo p. 26–29 · 1798-02-05 (fwd.; rep. 5 Jan 1798) · Savage abstract · 

Mission San Francisco's LAND-SHORTAGE representation (church-vs-state): citing the *Recopilación de Indias* (lib. 6 tít. 3 ley 1; lib. 4 tít. 12 ley 9, protecting Indian lands), Mission San Francisco [Dolores] is 'escasísima de tierras,' hemmed by the Almejas cove/ocean (W), the Port/Golden Gate (N), the Estero de Suisun [SF Bay] (E), land only to the S, sharing the 2-league peninsula with the presidio; the Guardian asks the Viceroy to order the Governor to REMOVE the presidio's cattle ranch (*ganado mayor*) as 'tan perjudicial á los pobres indios y á su Misión,' and to address lands given to *particulares* — reclaiming land in the Indians' name
