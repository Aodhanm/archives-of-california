# Vallejo's battle report of his (2nd, successful) 1829 campaign — the ESTANISLAO REVOLT — set out 26 May w/ 107 armed men

**C-A 53 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n11–15** · Sav p. 12–16 · tomo p. 24–36 · 1829-06-04 · Savage abstract · 

Vallejo's battle report of his (2nd, successful) 1829 campaign — the ESTANISLAO REVOLT — set out 26 May w/ 107 armed men + a cannon + 2,500 cartridges; burned the rebels' wooded river stronghold, artillery firing *bala rasa* + *metralla* across the river; the Indians fought from interconnected pits & trenches; a 2nd stronghold stormed (25 breaches cut) — artillery to 10 varas, 31 grapeshot rounds in 4 hrs, the wood ablaze; final tally 11 men + 2 Indian auxiliaries wounded (5 serious), several rebels + 3 women killed, 3 women captured, 18 stolen mission horses recovered; retired to Mission San José for want of ammunition
