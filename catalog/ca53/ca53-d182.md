# the US-conquest transition — under the new American regime, Vallejo transmits to Kearny copies of the Figueroa instructi

**C-A 53 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n323–326** · Sav p. 324–327 · tomo p. 405–408 (Sav. pp. 686–92) · 1847-04-05 · Savage abstract · 

the US-conquest transition — under the new American regime, Vallejo transmits to Kearny copies of the Figueroa instructions that authorized his colonization of the northern frontier & the founding of a *ciudad* in the Valley of Sonoma: he alone empowered to grant land there, charged (a 'Muy Reservado' note) to make 'la población Mexicana preponderar á la extranjera' against the risk from 'los vecinos del Norte,' mindful of 'la importancia del Puerto de Bodega y Cabo Mendocino… para la conservación de la integridad Nacional.' The irony: Vallejo hands this secret anti-foreign-encroachment charter to the conquering American general
