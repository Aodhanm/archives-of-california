# Monterey's protest against the 1835 decree moving the Territory's capital to Los Ángeles — argues Monterey has been capi

**C-A 53 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n265–272** · Sav p. 266–273 · tomo p. 347–354 · 1835-10-12 · Savage abstract · 

Monterey's protest against the 1835 decree moving the Territory's capital to Los Ángeles — argues Monterey has been capital since founding (10+ yrs' 'capitalidad'), a frequented port with water, wood, timber for an arsenal & a possible *dársena*, more populous, 'más culta,' central, benign-climed & the grain-supplying North near the Russian/American-coveted frontier; Los Ángeles is 10 leagues inland, worse, 'menos cultura,' only the unsafe San Pedro roadstead — and moving would cost 30,000+ pesos. Names Carrillo (LA) & Bandini (S. Diego) as 'primer motor' of the change; asks the Cámaras to suspend/revoke the LA-capital law + grant Monterey the title of *ciudad*. Drafted by José Joaquín González, committee Hartnell & Franco Pacheco, approved 13 Oct (Spence, Maldonado). The Monterey–LA capital rivalry
