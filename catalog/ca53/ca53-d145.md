# Monterey's protest against the 1835 decree moving the Territory's capital to Los Ángeles — argues Monterey has been capi

**C-A 53 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n265–267** · Sav p. 266–268+ · tomo p. 347–349+ · 1835-11-15 · Savage abstract · 

Monterey's protest against the 1835 decree moving the Territory's capital to Los Ángeles — argues Monterey has been capital since founding (10+ yrs' 'capitalidad'), is a frequented port with water, wood, timber for an arsenal & a possible *dársena*, more populous & 'más culta'; Los Ángeles is 10 leagues inland, worse-built, 'menos cultura,' with only the distant, unsafe San Pedro roadstead ('no es Puerto ni Bahía'). The Monterey–LA capital rivalry
