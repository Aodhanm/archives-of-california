# the 1845 proposals shaping the missions' fate (prelude to Pico's mission sales): the Government to demand exact credit/d

**C-A 53 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n316–317** · Sav p. 317–318 · tomo p. 398–399 (Sav. pp. 617–8) · 1845-04-19 · Savage abstract · 

the 1845 proposals shaping the missions' fate (prelude to Pico's mission sales): the Government to demand exact credit/debt accounts from every mission; suspend land grants adjoining the missions (reserve some for ejidos); suspend issuing neophytes their 'carta de libertad' pending decisive measures; and have the padres abstain from selling the missions' useful goods
