# Durán's substantial neophyte-property treatise (the reply Doc 157 answered): defends the padres (fault mere 'sorpresa é 

**C-A 53 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n292–301** · Sav p. 293–302 · tomo p. 374–383 (Sav. pp. 635–8) · 1836-06-15 · Savage abstract · 

Durán's substantial neophyte-property treatise (the reply Doc 157 answered): defends the padres (fault mere 'sorpresa é inadvertencia'), then argues the mission goods 'son propiedad legal de los Neófitos' — the Indians 'los han sudado, los han criado con sus propios brazos' — so the government has no more right to requisition them than any private person's property; the missionaries are mere 'tutores' of the neophyte communities (their goods like a *menor*'s, regulable but not seizable). Clinches it by quoting the Supreme Government's own 19 Oct 1831 decision (re: the S. Luis Obispo síndico Martiarena) that 'los bienes de las Misiones… no deben ser tenidos por bienes de la Hacienda pública'; auxilios are owed only by 'necesidad urgente,' not 'rigorosa de justicia' — though in practice the missions never refused, often under threats. ⚠ also indicts Híjar's mismanagement (50,000 p of needless salaries 'sobre las propiedades de los mismos indios')
