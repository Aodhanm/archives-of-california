# the eve of the US conquest — on José Mª Covarrubias's mission to Mexico City (a detailed exposition on the Californias' 

**C-A 53 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n329–332** · Sav p. 330–333 · tomo p. 411–414 (Sav. pp. 699–712) · 1846-04-23 · Savage abstract · 

the eve of the US conquest — on José Mª Covarrubias's mission to Mexico City (a detailed exposition on the Californias' precarious state): the President, aware California is 'codiciada de algunas Naciones extranjeras,' reports an expedition already 'en camino' with War/Treasury providences so the Department can 'repeler cualquier intento del exterior.' Touches Sutter's fort on the Sacramento (to be removed), Stephen (Esteban) Smith & colonization, Híjar's 1845 commission, a deferred Sandwich-Islands (Hawaii) consul; orders Pío Pico to continue serving; forwarded to Subprefect Abel Stearns (LA)
