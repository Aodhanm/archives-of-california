# the arbitrios committee opines that the missions — though still named 'Misiones' — are legally pueblos of the Republic (

**C-A 53 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n173–174** · Sav p. 175–176 · tomo p. 263–264 (Sav. pp. 368–9) · 1834-10-30 · Savage abstract · 

the arbitrios committee opines that the missions — though still named 'Misiones' — are legally pueblos of the Republic (they 'should already be secularized by law') and must therefore pay *propios y arbitrios* like all other inhabitants; proposes the *reducciones/pueblos de indígenas* stand subject to the *cabecera-de-partido* political authority; approved by Figueroa & the Diputación 30 Oct 1834
