# report of the FIRST (failed) expedition against Estanislao, May 1829 — the rebels fortified on the Río de los Laquisimes

**C-A 53 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n15–20** · Sav p. 16–21 · tomo p. 37–44 · 1829-05-16 · Savage abstract · 

report of the FIRST (failed) expedition against Estanislao, May 1829 — the rebels fortified on the Río de los Laquisimes (Stanislaus R.) in an 'impregnable wood'; the *pedrero*'s carriage broke on the 1st shot; ⭐⭐⭐ Estanislao (named 'cabecilla principal') came to parley but refused to surrender ('había provocado… y morirían'); the neophytes returned arrows and firearms; 4 soldiers broke ranks to steal horses → 2 soldiers killed & beheaded, bodies thrown in the river; final: 2 dead + 8 wounded (3 serious) + 3 auxiliaries (1 mortal), armament ruined; commends cabo Antonio Soto, soldiers Manuel Piña & Tomás Pacheco
