# Echeandía's long apologia DEFENDING his 6 Jan 1831 secularization bando (a major statement of liberal-secularization ide

**C-A 53 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n38–57** · Sav p. 39–58 · tomo p. 131–166 · 1834-05-17 · Savage abstract · 

Echeandía's long apologia DEFENDING his 6 Jan 1831 secularization bando (a major statement of liberal-secularization ideology, n38–50+): (a) legal argument — the 1813 Cortes decree has TWO aspects, ecclesiastical (Divine Worship) vs. temporal government (binding on the CIVIL authorities) — so as Jefe Político he was *obliged* to secularize the temporal side; (b) quotes his 1825 instructions licensing gradual liberal reform ('las ventajas del Sistema liberal… con prudencia'); (c) walks the bando article-by-article; (d) indicts mission abuses — neophytes 'deprived of personal liberty and the fruit of their labor'; (e) ⭐⭐⭐ his stated 6-year goal: 'convertir en hombres libres y propietarios á los 8,000 forzados indígenas reducidos en las 21 Misiones,' then civilize the gentiles — growing the population & securing the borders 'sin necesidad de colonizar por esta parte con extranjeros' (whose loyalty 'nunca será tan segura'; cf. New Mexico & Texas 'bastante temible'); (f) rebuts the federal charge (Doc 27) that he usurped the Indians' faculty — his bando's Art. 2 had the ayuntamiento name the administrators; acted w/ the Diputación's consent, 'in favor of los reducidos'; (g) ⭐⭐⭐ 'los bienes de las misiones son de los reducidos en ellas' (mission property belongs to the neophytes — the ground for distributing it to them; invokes Neve's 1781 Reglamento); (h) a 2nd population figure — ~13,000 reducidos ≈ ⅘ of the Territory (vs the 8,000 'forzados'); aim to 'arruinar de una vez el poder de los Misioneros'; ties in Herrera's fiscal abuse + friar-royalism/Solís revolt (ends n57 — a ~35-page apologia, tomo pp.131–166)
