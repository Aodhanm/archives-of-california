# THE Híjar Instructions — 'Instrucciones á que deberá arreglar su conducta D.n José Mª de Híjar, Gefe Político de la Alta

**C-A 53 (State Papers, Missions and Colonization), scan n180–183** · Sav p. 182–185 · tomo p. 270–273 (Sav. pp. 383–6) · 1834-04-23 · verbatim transcription · 

THE Híjar Instructions — 'Instrucciones á que deberá arreglar su conducta D.n José Mª de Híjar, Gefe Político de la Alta California y Director de la Colonización de esta y de la Baja.' Art. 1: 'Dará principio por ocupar todos los bienes pertenecientes á las Misiones de ambas Californias' (occupy ALL mission property of both Californias; the military commander to furnish force); one-year term; colonists' town-lots (≤ 500 varas, solares within the traced street-lines); each colonist family gets in full ownership *caballerías* of land (1 if irrigated / 16 if grazing), 4 cows, 2 ox-teams or 2 bulls, 2 tame horses, 4 colts, 4 fillies, small stock + 12 fitted plows; no family sold > 200 head of one class (art. 12); half the surplus mission goods reserved by the general govt for worship, missionary *congrua*, primary-schoolteachers' pay & free farm tools for colonists (art. 13); Híjar to render annual accounts of mission-property products & the colony's state (arts. 14–15). The contested mission-occupation mandate Figueroa refused to execute — the legal heart of the Híjar–Padrés affair. ⚠ corrects the gap-sample's page estimate ('tomo pp. 370–371' → actual tomo pp. 270–273 / Sav. pp. 383–6)
