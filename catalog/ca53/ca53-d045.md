'Informe RESERVADO sobre el Establecimiento de Ross' — Vallejo's first-person military reconnaissance of the Russian coast/interior (EXPANDED-ENTRY CANDIDATE). Part 1 (n97–104): left Ross the 28th, reached Puerto de Bodega (7 leguas off) = 4 wooden factory-buildings the Russians built to serve their ships/foreigners and export what they extract; the Bodega + 'San Francisco alias Los Tamales' bay geography (navigable, small craft 6 leguas inland); ⭐⭐⭐ at Bodega permanently only 'una Capitanía de gentiles' — 43 people under Captain *Guchactac* — whose purpose is to GUARD the Russian factories (unmolested by other Indians; San Rafael mission-Indian scouts, 'un tal Rafael'); ⭐⭐ Indian grievances/intelligence (soldiers luring gentiles & fugitive Christians then imprisoning them → San Rafael/Sonoma to Christianize; Indians 'resueltos á morir' — ⚠ rough paleography, transcribe before quoting); rode inland to Santa Rosa (12 leguas from Bodega) — the arroyo where the Russians farmed 'dos años hace' (~1831), depopulated rancherías (Selayomi), irrigable lands, ⭐ 'abunda de castores' (beaver/otter) + 'vestigios de los antiguos pescadores'; ⭐⭐⭐ Santa Rosa/Jaquiximi has 'las mejores circunstancias para la fundación de un pueblo' (the reconnaissance behind the Sonoma-frontier counter-colonization); his Indian-pacification diplomacy; ⭐⭐⭐ a denunciation of the missionaries' 'mala fé, maltrato y crueldad… sistema sanguinario… ¡Qué monstruosidades!' as the root of frontier-Indian hostility.
