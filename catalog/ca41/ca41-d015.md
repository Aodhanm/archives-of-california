# ✓ FULLY VERIFIED vs scans n19–n22 (2026-07-06): date/parties, the '9 Nov 1824' Monterey contract, $14,000 round-trip fre

**C-A 41 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Custom House)), scan n19–n22** · Sav p. 19–22 · tomo p. 18–21 (orig. 141–7) · 1826-08-01 · Savage abstract · 

✓ FULLY VERIFIED vs scans n19–n22 (2026-07-06): date/parties, the '9 Nov 1824' Monterey contract, $14,000 round-trip freight, the $10,000 'libre de cámara' clause, and — verbatim on n20 — 'las 375 pieles de nutria en Canton… á $18¾ una con otra' all confirmed. *(This $18¾ Canton resale is the figure cross-referenced for the [[ca3-prov-state-papers-v-vi-survey]] Doc 445 otter-tariff correction — now independently verified.)* The Cooper/*Rover* otter-contract accounting. Arbitration of the pending accounts from the contract Gov. Luis A. Argüello made (auth. by the Diputación) with Capt. John R. Cooper to send the schr *Rover* to Canton with government otter skins. Panel: Cooper, Juan Bandini (govt), Wm A. Richardson (interpreter/price-expert), w/ Cristóbal Palomares & José A. Carrillo (diputados). 375 skins sold in Canton @ $18¾ each; effects delivered to Domingo Carrillo (habilitado, San Diego). Full contract (Monterey, 9 Nov 1824): Cooper to fit *Rover* for an Asia voyage sailing early Jan 1825; province pays $14,000 round-trip freight; $10,000 duty-free ('libre de cámara'); vessel not to leave Canton overloaded. Echeandía declares the Canton sale/costs 'no plenamente justificada' but leaves liability on Cooper for the Supreme Govt to judge.
