# Acknowledges the Gov.'s approval (oficio of the 18th) of the *Lydia* capture; explains why he could NOT capture the *Alb

**C-A 41 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Custom House)), scan n10** · Sav p. 10–15 · tomo p. i.9–15 (encl. to i.40) · 1816-01-22 · Savage abstract · 

Acknowledges the Gov.'s approval (oficio of the 18th) of the *Lydia* capture; explains why he could NOT capture the *Albatross* — insufficient force, she had cannon & ~12 men, lay ~2 mi offshore, he would not risk a rash action against viceregal interests; his poor 'intelig.a en asuntos navales'; put a guard aboard the *Lydia* so nothing be unloaded; boasts neither troops nor settlers took 'una aguja' of foreign property; his reasons the *Albatross* will return (passport ashore, no food, only 7 men & no pilot left); Capt. Smith 'el más pobre de los que han venido á esta costa.' Encloses the *Lydia*'s passports, register, and the Anglo-American peace proclamation. *(Savage runs several Guerra enclosures together, tomo pp. 9–40.)*
