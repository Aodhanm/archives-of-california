# English letter ⚠️ that survives ONLY as a translation 'made by Ivan Petroff' (Savage's closing note, n87/p.88, verbatim:

**C-A 56 (State Papers, Sacramento), scan n83–87** · Sav p. 84–88 · tomo p. 506–509 · 1833-07-24 · Savage abstract · 

English letter ⚠️ that survives ONLY as a translation 'made by Ivan Petroff' (Savage's closing note, n87/p.88, verbatim: *'The communication on the 4 preceding pages is a translation from the Russian original — made by Ivan Petroff'* — the Russian original is NOT in the volume) rebutting Capt. Beechey's (HMS *Blossom*, 1826–27) accusations that the Russians seized islands off Santa Bárbara & poached sea-otter depriving the natives — Wrangel calls it 'untrue and fabricated,' insists the RAC obeyed Mexico's otter-hunting prohibition, and pins the poaching on 'other foreigners' using 'some forty canoes' of 'our northern savages' (Aleut hunters) carrying on sea-otter hunting on the coasts of Upper & Lower California; seeks to restore Russo-Californian friendship. ✓ transcription verified accurate to the leaves (n83–n85, n87), 2026-07-05 verify pass — BUT ⚠️⚠️ this is *Petroff's* English, not Wrangel's words: Ivan Petroff has a documented forgery/embellishment record → do NOT quote as Wrangel's own voice; collate against a Russian-archives text before citing. See [[petroff-translation-reliability]].
