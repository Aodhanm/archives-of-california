# the DIARY of the June 1823 expedition to reconnoiter the site for Mission San Francisco Solano (Sonoma) — Days 1–6 (25–3

**C-A 56 (State Papers, Sacramento), scan n144–153** · Sav p. 145–154+ · tomo p. XI·(from ~330) · 1823-07-06 · verbatim transcription · 

the DIARY of the June 1823 expedition to reconnoiter the site for Mission San Francisco Solano (Sonoma) — Days 1–6 (25–30 Jun): SF → San Rafael → Olompali → Petaluma plain → Laguna de Tolay (named for Coast Miwok chief Tolay) → ⭐⭐ 'el plano llamado Sonoma, de los indios que en otro tiempo vivían en él' (the founding site — oak groves, streams, redwood & fir, a hot spring, salmon in the estero, ⭐ thermometer 11° warmer than SF, 'bien apto para fundar una misión') → the Napa & ⭐⭐ Suisun plains (both named for their former Indian inhabitants); ⭐ Indians light signal fires on sighting the party, the grasslands burned (Indian land management); ⭐⭐ a diplomatic feast — 14 Indians (5 captains) of a Suisun-area ranchería summoned via 3 Christian neophytes & fed pinole/venison 'para preparar una conquista'; rancherías named Petaluma, Libantelegumí, Ululato, Libaytos[?]; gift-diplomacy (cotton shirts, beads, tallow) + conversion exhortation; 3 bears killed; Days 7–9 (1–3 Jul) — Petaluma rejected 'del todo seco,' Sonoma confirmed ('un manantial de manantiales'); ⭐⭐⭐ the FOUNDING DECISION — the escort cmdr José Sánchez, provincial deputy Francisco Castro, & Fr. José Altimira agree to place the mission at Sonoma & set 'la Santa Cruz, primer cimiento,' w/ a strategic-frontier logic (a rancho at Petaluma as a 'ladder' to the NW gentility + one at Napa to 'fence off' the NE gentiles); ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Day 10 (4 Jul 1823) — THE FOUNDING: a field altar; a redwood Holy Cross (7 varas tall) blessed & raised 'dentro del sitio en donde los Gentiles de Sonoma tenían antiguamente su ranchería'; the troop fires a volley; Sánchez & Altimira sing the *Lignum Crucis* & *Crux fidelis* w/ the neophytes; Mass; named 'el nuevo San Francisco (Solano)'; back at the SF presidio by 6 Jul (spans n144–162, signed José Sánchez)
