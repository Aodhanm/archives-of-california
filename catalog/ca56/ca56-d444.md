# major church-state / contraband treatise defending taxing the missions — invokes Art. 338 of the 1812 Constitution (cont

**C-A 56 (State Papers, Sacramento), scan n381–385** · Sav p. 382–386 · tomo p. 481–487 · 1821-05-19 · Savage abstract · 

major church-state / contraband treatise defending taxing the missions — invokes Art. 338 of the 1812 Constitution (contribuciones not alcabalas); the missions supplied $400,000 in libranzas (seeds, lard, soap) since the 1810 insurrection; accuses the missions of contraband resale (goods 'not for the neophytes but to profit') unloaded at hidden coves (Cojo, Rancho de Ortega, San Pedro, San Juan Capistrano); threatens to appoint a customs inspector (frigate *Rita* owing $6,000–8,000); 'casi todos los intereses… pertenecen á los indios'; laments the troops receive not even ¼ their pay
