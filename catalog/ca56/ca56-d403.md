# on whether the old prohibitions on trade with foreigners in Spanish-American ports have expired — the Mex. papers show s

**C-A 56 (State Papers, Sacramento), scan n350–352** · Sav p. 351–353 · tomo p. XVIII·25–28 · 1822-06-11 · Savage abstract · 

on whether the old prohibitions on trade with foreigners in Spanish-American ports have expired — the Mex. papers show ships of many nations now trading & paying duties; foreigners may settle in Mexican territory by embracing Catholicism; will petition the Cortes to admit foreign artisans ('con la mezcla de hombres laboriosos') to cure the inhabitants' *dexidad*; the govt won't raise import/export duties (the Prov. has few *arbitrios*); ⭐⭐⭐ 'Guillermo, compañero de D.n Luis Macala' [= William Hartnell, partner of Hugh McCulloch] wishes to contract with the missions for 25,000 @ (arrobas) de sebos, cueros de res y otros productos' (the founding McCulloch-Hartnell hide-&-tallow proposal) — ✎ quantity CORRECTED 40,000→25,000 at full res 2026-07-05 (verify pass; the numeral matches the '25' of the same leaf's 'pp. 25–8'); Savage's own '(sic)' follows 'Macala' in the copy; + recovered detail: the day before, the Gov. went 'á visitar á Luis Quastara [? unidentified — Savage phonetic] en Misión de Sⁿ Carlos'
