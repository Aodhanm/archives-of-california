# ⭐⭐⭐ Causa criminal — the poisoning of Fr. Pedro Panto by his Indian cook Nazario (Mission San Diego). Cabo Antonio Guill

**C-A 17 (Ben. Mil. XL–LII, 1809–22), scan n190–n195** · Sav p. 191–196 · 1811-12-18 (events Nov 1811) · verbatim transcription · 

⭐⭐⭐ Causa criminal — the poisoning of Fr. Pedro Panto by his Indian cook Nazario (Mission San Diego). Cabo Antonio Guillén & Sgt. Mariano Mercado noticed the friar's soup tasted bitter (16 Nov); Panto vomited badly. Nazario confessed he'd carried a poison herb ("escoba amarga") for days and put it in the soup because Panto flogged him relentlessly — by his account 50 + 24 + 25 + 25 lashes over 15–16 Nov (200+ in two days "sin dar graves motivos") — "por ver si de este modo descansaba yo y los demás indios de la Misión"; testified "ningún indio de la Misión lo quiere, ni menos los gentiles; y al Padre Sánchez tanto lo quieren los cristianos como los gentiles." Defensor Pico: the herb wasn't lethal, Nazario "un indio bruto," driven by desperation. Fiscal Carrillo finds him not deserving of execution — asks 8 months' presidio at San Diego "para escarmiento de los demás." A landmark primary source on mission corporal punishment & indigenous resistance. Orig. pp.101–29
