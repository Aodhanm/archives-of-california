# ⭐⭐⭐ Long *borrador* register of Solá's outgoing letters/orders (1815–1822) — a major composite source. 1815–17 highlight

**C-A 17 (Ben. Mil. XL–LII, 1809–22), scan n117–n138** · Sav p. 118–139 · 1815–1822 (borradores; orig pp.152–350) · verbatim transcription · 

⭐⭐⭐ Long *borrador* register of Solá's outgoing letters/orders (1815–1822) — a major composite source. 1815–17 highlights: ⭐⭐⭐ the 5 neophyte murderers of Fr. Andrés Quintana (Lino, Antonino, Vicente, Saturnino & Fulgencio[?] of Sta Cruz) sentenced (10 Mar 1816) to 200 azotes + presidio (10 yrs the first two, 7 the others), at Sta Bárbara — resolution of the Quintana murder (cf. Doc 153); ⭐⭐ Solá commissions Gabriel Moraga, 25 men, to reconnoiter the sierra passes (18 Oct 1816); ⭐ a 3-masted brig built at San Juan Capistrano (Capt. Guillermo/William Reynolds); *cimarrón* pursuit & a San Gabriel uprising; SD soldiers Franco/Fermín/Fernando tried for the homicide of the Mayordomo Miguel; Vicente Nevado poisoning his wife; José Antº Tapia raping a neophyte; warship *San Carlos* + corbeta *Paz y Religión*; request for 40 mission Indians. 1819–20 highlights (the post-Bouchard reinforcement & Moraga's Mojave campaign): ⭐⭐ arrival at San Luis of the Mazatlán company — brig *Cossack* w/ 100 men + $10,000, Capt. Pablo de la Portilla (+ corbeta *Reina de los Ángeles*, *San Carlos*); promotions: Mariano Estrada (Tte.) & Manuel Gómez (artillery); ⭐⭐ Solá orders Gabriel Moraga's 1819 punitive campaign to the Mojave (Amajabat/Mongallune) ranchería — 35 cavalry + 15 Mazatlán infantry under Tte. Narciso Fabregat "para conocer á los gentiles la bravura de las armas del Rey" (revenge-raids by Colorado-River gentiles had hit San Buenaventura, taking 5 Christian captives); ⭐ Moraga couldn't reach Amajabat for lack of pasture, halting at San Joaquín & Santa Ana de Aguablanca, returning via San Gabriel (Antonio Mª Lugo alcalde); to resume next summer. 1820–21 highlights: ⭐ English whaler at the coast (Aug 1820, 36 crew, 200 barrels whale oil, "fragata inglesa ballenera," Capt. Guillermo [William] —; her captain caroused with Capt. Francisco Mª Ruiz, who was removed from the SD command/habilitación "por el uso de ciertos licores", replaced by José Mª Estudillo); foreigner-vigilance warnings; ⭐⭐ death of Cadet José Guadalupe Moraga at San Gabriel (Mar 1821); the Cocomaricopa "general" sent home in peace (the gentiles hostile at San Buenaventura 2 yrs prior); murder of the Mazatlán soldier Pablo Serna & the neophyte Apolonia; continued Amajaba/Mojave alarms (gentiles plotting to draw off the Christians of San Gabriel & San Fernando); Estudillo's 40-yr service/seniority dispute; ⭐ the American schooner *Eagle* suspected of clandestine trade (Fabregat sold it goods — foreign commerce prohibited); the Cocomaricopa "general" returned to his patria; command reshuffle (Ruiz→SD, Portilla→Sta Bárbara); ends w/ the death of Fr. Joaquín Pascual Nuez (San Gabriel, 1822). Orig. pp.152–350
