# ⭐ Causa criminal — uxoricide by a neophyte (CLOSED). On 4 Aug 1817 Cabo Antonio Olivera of the Sta Buenaventura escort r

**C-A 17 (Ben. Mil. XL–LII, 1809–22), scan n320–n323** · Sav p. 320–323 · 1817-08 → 1819-03-02 (dictamen) · verbatim transcription · 

⭐ Causa criminal — uxoricide by a neophyte (CLOSED). On 4 Aug 1817 Cabo Antonio Olivera of the Sta Buenaventura escort reported the indio Gorgonio had killed his wife, buried her, then taken refuge in the church (iglesia) [ecclesiastical asylum]; the indio Vitorio named accomplice (relayed by soldier José Vicente Pico). Body found "con 5 heridas y la cabeza muy maltratada." 5 Aug Tte. Guerra ordered Maitorena's sumaria. Defender Ignacio Martínez 10 Nov 1818; returned to fiscal 2 Mzo 1819; Gorgonio cited por edictos. Dictamen fiscal (Martínez): neophyte Gorgonio confessed strangling his own wife, afternoon of 3 Aug 1817 → digno de severo castigo; urges the Tribunal weigh "el derramamiento de la sangre inocente"; notes a parallel atrocious killing at Mission San Fernando, 22 leguas distant, plus others "quedado impunes" for difficulty of proving culprits — argues exemplary punishment is needed to deter these crimes "entre estos naturales." "(No se encuentra la defensa)" — defense not in the record. Orig. pp.417–453.
