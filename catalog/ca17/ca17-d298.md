# ⭐⭐⭐ Results of Moraga's 1819 campaign: Moraga captured fugitive Christian Indians (cimarrones) + 11 gentiles, sent to St

**C-A 17 (Ben. Mil. XL–LII, 1809–22), scan n238–n239** · Sav p. 239–240 · 1819-11-15 · verbatim transcription · 

⭐⭐⭐ Results of Moraga's 1819 campaign: Moraga captured fugitive Christian Indians (cimarrones) + 11 gentiles, sent to Sta Bárbara. Solá's sentences per named runaway by length of absence: Sebastián & Cristóbal 2 yrs; Ignacio, Cayetano, Diego 2 mo. + 3×25 lashes; Juan Mª (La Purísima) 2 yrs; Olegario (Sta Inés) 6 mo. + lashes; Felipe (San Miguel) 6 mo. + 25 lashes; the 11 gentile "harborers" (old men) 25 lashes × 3 days + 1 yr (to be Christianized). ⭐⭐ Per de la Guerra, gentile chiefs guided Moraga's expedition, extracted 11 Christians from the laguna de Buenavista (Buena Vista Lake, Tulares), offered to help soldiers enter "the islands" — Moraga paid them $2–3 each. Key on mission flight/runaways & the Tulares. Orig. pp.445–6
