# ⭐ Causa criminal into a homicide by Mission San Francisco neophytes — Jacobo (principal author), Apolinario, and the bli

**C-A 17 (Ben. Mil. XL–LII, 1809–22), scan n1–n10** · Sav p. 2–11 · 1809-04-15 → ruling 1810-10-10 (orig. pp.2–68) · verbatim transcription · 

⭐ Causa criminal into a homicide by Mission San Francisco neophytes — Jacobo (principal author), Apolinario, and the blind Tenbis/Toribio (accomplices): Jacobo beat Ebacio[?] to death from jealousy over a woman he wished to marry. Estudillo's long *defensa*: the accused are "recién convertidos," rustic Indians; the missionary ministers themselves flog neophytes "12, 15 ó 25 azotes" for woman-theft/adultery/homicide; pleads not for acquittal but for exemplary *destierro de Provincias* instead of death. Outcome: convicted on confession; case routed Arrillaga→Viceroy→*auditor de guerra* twice (defect: no *curador* at the neophytes' ratification); Toribio died at Mission SF 28 Nov 1809, attended by Fray Ramón Abella; auditor (28 Sep 1810) praised Estudillo's defensa "muy buena y útil"; sentence: 10 years' presidio at Monterey + afflictive lashes (Apolinario partly exempted); approved by Viceroy Venegas, 10 Oct 1810. Rich on mission discipline, neophyte legal status & indigenous custom.
