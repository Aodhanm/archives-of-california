# ⭐⭐ Third investigation — the stolen trunk & household concealment. Adds the theft of a baúl of valuables Fr. Olbés entru

**C-A 17 (Ben. Mil. XL–LII, 1809–22), scan n285–n290+** · Sav p. 286–291+ · 1819-03-23 · verbatim transcription · 

⭐⭐ Third investigation — the stolen trunk & household concealment. Adds the theft of a baúl of valuables Fr. Olbés entrusted for transport to Sta Clara — José Antº Robles & son Nieves stole it after getting Martín Ortega drunk; Ortega sold some contents (→ to be charged & arrested). Solá orders interrogating the mission Indians (about what they & the mayordomo hid) and the villagers' wives (concealed goods, cloth cut into clothes, items sold & to whom). Testimony: the wives (Pascuala Lorenzana, María Remigia Vázquez…) deny concealment; the Indian Silvestre and soldier José Mª Martín Ortega testify the mayordomo Castro directed the Indians to break doors with axes/bars and loot the warehouse & church ornaments, hauling goods to the laguna del Sargento. A multi-party plunder where villagers, Indians & mayordomo blame each other. Closes with the stolen-trunk strand: the Robles family (José Antº, Nieves, Rafael) sold the loot (a saya, black stockings, beads, blue-baize trousers) in San Francisco. Orig. pp.60–94
