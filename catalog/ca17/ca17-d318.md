# ⭐⭐⭐ "Declaraciones de insurgentes de la expedición de Bouchard" — depositions of the 4 captured/surrendered crew of Bouc

**C-A 17 (Ben. Mil. XL–LII, 1809–22), scan n254–n260** · Sav p. 255–261 · 1818-12-29 · verbatim transcription · 

⭐⭐⭐ "Declaraciones de insurgentes de la expedición de Bouchard" — depositions of the 4 captured/surrendered crew of Bouchard's frigates, revealing the multinational makeup of the expedition: (1) Pedro Saldívar, 22, of Buenos Aires, cabo; (2) Juan de la Rosa, 27, of Scotland, labrador; (3) Nicolás Blanco, 21, of Santa Fé, soldier of the frigate *Consecuencia*; (4) Mateo José Pascual, ~25, native of Africa, panadero, baptized in Buenos Aires. All questioned on Isidro Molina — uniformly testify he was drunk at the Monterey presidio, riding a white horse, not a prisoner, who came over voluntarily ("se ha venido á pasar á presentar"), with a rumor he was a spy (*vigía*) sent by the Governor; couldn't say if he landed at the Ortegas' rancho or went ashore willingly at Sta Bárbara. Each signed with a cross (illiterate). A remarkable primary source on Bouchard's crew & the Molina case. Orig. pp.~256–61
