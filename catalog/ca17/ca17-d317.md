# ⭐ Causa criminal — the neophyte Juan Bautista, who took church asylum at San Gabriel, accused of killing a Christian Ind

**C-A 17 (Ben. Mil. XL–LII, 1809–22), scan n252–n254** · Sav p. 253–255 · 1818-09-30 · verbatim transcription · 

⭐ Causa criminal — the neophyte Juan Bautista, who took church asylum at San Gabriel, accused of killing a Christian Indian (Lerme[?]) with arrows; both were mission runaways, the killing over a debt of "tres reales de abalorios" (3 reales of beads). Defense (the "mercader viandante" Juan Salvador Astorga): "un cristiano nuevo, rudo, y loco." Fiscal asks to contest the church asylum (per Benedict XIV's exclusion of such crimes). Orig. pp.810–39
