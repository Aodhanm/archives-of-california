# ⭐⭐⭐ Causa criminal — the 1820 interrogation of the neophyte Alberto (Mission Sta Cruz) re the murder of Fr. Andrés Quint

**C-A 17 (Ben. Mil. XL–LII, 1809–22), scan n247–n248+** · Sav p. 248–249+ · 1820-11-21 · verbatim transcription · 

⭐⭐⭐ Causa criminal — the 1820 interrogation of the neophyte Alberto (Mission Sta Cruz) re the murder of Fr. Andrés Quintana. Alberto's sworn testimony: the Indian Quirico invited him "á ayudar á matar al Padre," luring him to the mission huerta at prayer-time on the pretext of stealing pears, where the other conspirators were gathered; the Indian Andrés confirmed they meant to kill the Padre; Alberto says he refused ("que él no se metía en nada"), left for home, learned next morning the Padre was dead; confessed he did wrong not to warn the guard/mayordomo; fled to the *Monte*/sierra when the others were arrested, fed once by his son via Fr. Marcelino, told to go to Punta de Año Nuevo (the mission rancho). A rich primary indigenous-testimony source on the Quintana assassination (cf. Docs 153/177/188). Orig. pp.707–10
