# ⭐⭐⭐ The Branciforte sack of Mission Santa Cruz during the Bouchard scare. Fr. Ramón Olbés complained the Branciforte com

**C-A 17 (Ben. Mil. XL–LII, 1809–22), scan n276–n278+** · Sav p. 276–278+ · 1818-12-08 (events Nov 1818; investig. Nov 1819) · verbatim transcription · 

⭐⭐⭐ The Branciforte sack of Mission Santa Cruz during the Bouchard scare. Fr. Ramón Olbés complained the Branciforte comisionado & villagers committed "atentados" on the mission's property. When Bouchard's force took Monterey (23 Nov 1818), Olbés evacuated the mission toward Sta Clara; the comisionado Joaquín Buelna had been ordered to remove the mission's goods (under inventory) before the enemy could sack them — instead, the inválido Joaquín Castro found Buelna & most of the Branciforte vecindario sacking the mission: forcing the main gate with axes, breaking the warehouse windows, carrying off the goods; returning 6–7 days later they found it "enteramente saqueada," doors/windows/stores broken, the church profaned — a crucifix, the image of San Luis & the Santo Niño "hechos pedazos," excrement on canes in the sacristy. A vivid, well-known episode. The testimony is conflicting: the comisionado Joaquín Buelna's defense quotes the Gov.'s actual written order ("hará V. que pase con algunos á destruir aquellos intereses para que los enemigos no se apoderen de ellos") and blames the mayordomo Castro & 60 mission Indians for the disorderly sack; villager José Mª Salazar says Castro broached the liquor barrels; some say the Indians (not the villagers) broke the crucifix and that San José/the Santo Niño were undamaged. Argüello sent all the declarations to the Gov. (18 Nov 1819). Orig. pp.3–26
