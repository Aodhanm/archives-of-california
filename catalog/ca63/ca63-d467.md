# THE VICENTA SÁNCHEZ AGUARDIENTE CASE: Stevenson (16 May) re Mrs. Vicenta [Sánchez] de Olvelle selling liquor at her tave

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n336–337** · Sav p. 334 · 1848-05-16→22 · Savage abstract · 

THE VICENTA SÁNCHEZ AGUARDIENTE CASE: Stevenson (16 May) re Mrs. Vicenta [Sánchez] de Olvelle selling liquor at her tavern — the punishment ought to be applied to her husband, for the reason that if she has broken the law it was to maintain her children, & the sentence referred to his approbation; Ortega, questioned (22 May), said Mrs. Vicenta Sánchez de Olvelle sold aguardiente to an Indian, which Ortega ordered her to appear before him, answering that she had been accustomed [to it] before other authorities of more importance than alcaldes, & that she again made several Indians drunk; summoned to appear, on the third summons she appeared & was fined $50 for having at 3 different times broken the Governor's liquor law; afterwards she presented an order from Col. Stevenson prohibiting the sentence from being carried into effect — ⭐ a woman prosecuted for the Indian-liquor trade, the military govt overriding the alcalde
