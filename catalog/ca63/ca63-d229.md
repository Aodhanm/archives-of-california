# His resignation & reasons: has endeavored to do his duty but finds it necessary to resign his commission — the law had b

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n129–130** · Sav p. 129 · 1847-12-05 · Savage abstract · 

His resignation & reasons: has endeavored to do his duty but finds it necessary to resign his commission — the law had been taken out of his hands by the Sub-Indian Agent, who established a court for the trial of cases at San Luis Rey, paying no attention to his protestations & solicitations to have those cases brought before him as being the proper authority; complains that in his instructions he was required to call, in case of need, for assistance on the military — has done so on two occasions & his applications were ignored, the effect of the Indian Agent's influence or of counter orders from higher authority; in either case the Alcalde's authority has become null; many abuses being committed, Indian depredations, & others who are not Indians; many suffer, among them some who have been good friends to the Americans
