# THE CASTRO SELF-VINDICATION: supposes that by this date Mason has received his communication dated at San Luis Obispo re

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n381–382** · Sav p. 379 · 1848-02-09 · Savage abstract · 

THE CASTRO SELF-VINDICATION: supposes that by this date Mason has received his communication dated at San Luis Obispo relative to his arrival at that point; today received Mason's letter of the 7th manifesting the informes given re a revolution in which Castro is said to be implicated; it may well be that the delay of his march gave his enemies occasion to detract from his reputation in that manner; assures Mason it is all an atrocious imposture, that tomorrow he will have the satisfaction of clearing himself in His Excellency's presence & testify to the authorities now governing the Dept of his sane intention in returning, & his gratitude for the kindnesses shown; assures he will always contribute all in his power to maintain the peace & tranquility of 'este país en que ví la luz primera y que amo como el primero de sus hijos'; 'los impostores no serán jamás capaces de mirar con sentimientos con el que tiene el honor de repetirse su sincero amigo y servidor' — ⭐⭐ José Castro's formal denial of being a conquest-era revolutionary conspirator
