# Re José, Chief of an Indian tribe [San Diego area]: appeared with some of his people, was kindly treated; said some of h

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n43** · Sav p. 43 · 1848-02-06 · Savage abstract · 

Re José, Chief of an Indian tribe [San Diego area]: appeared with some of his people, was kindly treated; said some of his neighbors were constantly committing depredations, stealing horses, women & children — he wished to wage war agt them; was told the Gov. would not sanction his making war, but if the other tribes attacked his villages, he certainly had the right of defense & of chastising the invaders whilst fighting, but prisoners must be brought to the Alcalde. 'José acquiesced — is a good, trusty Indian, quite influential, his people numerous'; also resigns his own office, believing very little chance of the men agreeing to remain till end of year
