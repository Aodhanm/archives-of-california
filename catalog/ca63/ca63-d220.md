# On trials by jury: referring to his decision in the case agt Pedro Badillo, says he tried & sentenced him without a jury

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n122** · Sav p. 122 · 1848-04-17 · Savage abstract · 

On trials by jury: referring to his decision in the case agt Pedro Badillo, says he tried & sentenced him without a jury, because in a former case of a man accused of selling liquor to the Indians (contrary to Gov's prohibition) he called a jury of 6 men, half foreigners & half Californians — there were two witnesses who clearly proved the theft, & yet his countrymen would not find him guilty; called another jury in same manner, ended like the first; so refusing to trust agt their own countrymen; eventually he sentenced the culprit to the lowest penalty but afterwards remitted part of the sentence
