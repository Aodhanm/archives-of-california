# THE SAN BUENAVENTURA ALCALDE DISPUTE: Ortega, alcalde at San Buenaventura, announced in April that since he received his

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n334–337** · Sav p. 332 · 1848-05 · Savage abstract · 

THE SAN BUENAVENTURA ALCALDE DISPUTE: Ortega, alcalde at San Buenaventura, announced in April that since he received his appointment under Aznar's control, he would not remain if another were to have charge; Stevenson removed him, appointing Isaac Callaghan in his place; Ortega regrets to interfere with the civil dept, but as Carrillo (& more than once ordered to obey his orders, esp. re the alcaldía of San Buenaventura) it is repeated he will suspend his functions & ask Mason his removal; a certification (5 Jun 1848) signed E. Addison, Diego Roth[?], Nic. A. Den, Guillº Barns, Carlos A. Carrillo, José Carrillo, Anastasio Carrillo, F.M. Covarrubias attesting Francisco Ortega is a laborious, active young man, faithful to his word, of fine feelings & good habits, esteemed by every person; Stevenson's proclamation appointing Callaghan alcalde of San Buenaventura; the removal made 'having received a declaration under oath in which he denied having renounced his office'
