# THE FOXEN TRIAL TESTIMONY (cont. of doc 485): defense by F.M. Leavenworth; the accused answered he was not guilty; witne

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n354–356** · Sav p. 352 · 1848-05 (trial); 1844-05-22 (crime) · verbatim transcription · 

THE FOXEN TRIAL TESTIMONY (cont. of doc 485): defense by F.M. Leavenworth; the accused answered he was not guilty; witness Luis Olivas declared: 'Salimos con Foxen de Santa Inés para Morán [él y Dávila], fuimos á dormir al Rancho de Roca... á sestear al Rancho del Señor Julián... el difunto ['Dávila'] nos dijo... trae Vd. aquel costal con bastimento...' — a detailed first-person narration of the party's journey & the confrontation over a rooster/hen (gallo/gallina) at Señor Julián's rancho, where the fatal shooting occurred; second witness Jesús Feliz (mujer de la víctima) corroborates: 'al amanecer Dávila... se acercó á Dávila soltando como los indios y gritando 'me están robando'... Foxen no se contuvo sino que tiró á Dávila con una pistola. En seguida cayó Dávila' — an eyewitness account of the homicide
