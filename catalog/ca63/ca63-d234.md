# On affairs of the pueblo: matters originally bad growing worse — large portion of the people lazy, addicted to gambling,

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n132–133** · Sav p. 132 · 1847-12-28 · Savage abstract · 

On affairs of the pueblo: matters originally bad growing worse — large portion of the people lazy, addicted to gambling, no visible means of livelihood, support themselves by stealing cattle or horses; neither the former Alcaldes (Burton & Weeks) command the respect an Alcalde ought; wanted an alcalde 'who is not afraid to do his duty and who knows what his duty is'; Capt. Fisher would make a very good Alcalde but couldn't be prevailed on to accept; the best one after him is Charles White; election of a new town council impossible; asks a promiscuous body of 25 men of U.S. troops be stationed there to guard the prison-officers & aid the alcaldes arresting refractory convicts
