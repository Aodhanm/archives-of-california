# THE STA BÁRBARA BOUNDARY & REPRESENTATION PROTEST: encloses an acta in which the Pueblo of Sta Bárbara resolved to make 

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n374–375** · Sav p. 372 · 1849-07-02 · Savage abstract · 

THE STA BÁRBARA BOUNDARY & REPRESENTATION PROTEST: encloses an acta in which the Pueblo of Sta Bárbara resolved to make observations on the 3-Jun-1849 Riley proclamation (the civil-govt plan of the country); a first observation objects to the new marking of California district limits, by which part of Sta Bárbara's inhabitants & extension of its population would pass to Los Ángeles & San Luis Obispo, to the detriment of their interests — the Pueblo asks the limits of Sta Bárbara on the south side remain as the Assembly marked them in 1846; a second observation: Sta Bárbara has not been given the representation due it at the Convention, while other districts of lesser population were assigned 4 or 5 delegates & Sta Bárbara only 2; objects to the appointment of supernumerary/suplente delegates; signed Pablo de la Guerra (Presidente), Antonio María de la Guerra & Raymundo Carrillo (Secretarios) — ⭐⭐ a Californio protest against under-representation at the 1849 constitutional convention
