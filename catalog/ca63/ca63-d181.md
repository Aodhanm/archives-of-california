# Can't send acct of customs rev'd in this country during 2nd & 3rd quarters 1847 — none was rec'd; from time people had c

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n84–85** · Sav p. 84 · 1847-09-27 · Savage abstract · 

Can't send acct of customs rev'd in this country during 2nd & 3rd quarters 1847 — none was rec'd; from time people had capitulated to Capt. Montgomery of U.S. ship Portsmouth till winter's arrival no business was done — no revenue, none to sustain the pub. auth. Collector at La Paz (Angel Loriya) & at San José (Miguel Eliozar[?]) each had salary of $700/yr; former secy of the Govt still employed by him (Burton) at $700/yr; interpreter at $50/mo. Created a 'Juez de 1ª Instancia.' 'By the politeness of Fray Ramirez y Arellano, Padre Presidente in this Peninsula,' gives estimate of population of Baja Cal. by jurisdiction: La Paz 2,000 souls, San Antº 1,000, Santiago 1,300, San José 3,000, Todos Santos 1,500, Loreto 1,000, Comondú/Muleje/S. Ignº 1,000 = 10,800; of these 200 are Indians very much scattered; in the portion between an E–W line passing thru San Ignacio & the frontiers of Alta Cal. the population is 2,000
