# About Indians & property of the mission [San Luis Rey]: on 25 Aug had meeting at San Luis Rey with Indians (abt 130 men 

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n92–93** · Sav p. 92 · 1847-09-01 · Savage abstract · 

About Indians & property of the mission [San Luis Rey]: on 25 Aug had meeting at San Luis Rey with Indians (abt 130 men & women), explaining thro' one of the chiefs, an educated Indian speaking Spanish, the intentions of U.S. Govt — they appeared well pleased with the appt of a Govt Ind. Agent among them, also with the assurance of their welfare; promised obedience to the Act, were assured of protection if well-behaved, & of punishment if they acted badly; assured of strict justice being done them; called 3 of their chiefs & named them 'who the Ministry [comisarios[?]] rightly thought had been entrusted could be found'; the San Indians have raised a large crop of corn, enough they all say to carry them thro' the winter; scattered at their settlements at Santa Margarita, San Luis, Temecula, Temaycall[?] &c — this on my way to Temecula & San Luis Rey; going by you & belong to another tribe, saw more evidence of comfortable living among them than [Sr Ml at] most of the ranchos of Californians who are regarded as rich men
