# THE VALLEJO FRONTIER-TRIBES REPORT: could not write sooner, occupied with the tribes of the north & the coast beyond thi

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n314–315** · Sav p. 314 · 1847-05-26 · Savage abstract · 

THE VALLEJO FRONTIER-TRIBES REPORT: could not write sooner, occupied with the tribes of the north & the coast beyond this point who have committed depredations in the immediate ranchos of Mesa & others very recently; sent runners to the tribes to warn that the govt wished peace, to hear & fairly redress complaints; a few days later a captain of Indians with 10 men, named Cucusuy, presented himself, & Vallejo gave back the articles taken from the Socoya tribe in the act of war; per information taken, the Indians armed to defend their women & children whom they [the whites] tried to take violently & criminally; from the interview Cucusuy promised to present himself within a month to the chief of the Tabuny[?] tribe to keep peace; small gifts given; + the Boggs-appointment vs Nash-reinstatement alcalde dispute at Sonoma — Boggs as alcalde produced resistance from the one who should hand over the post; public meetings for Nash's reinstatement, whom Gen. Kearny had no power to remove, a lawyer leading a faction of ignorant people & Nash himself; an inventory of the seized articles closes the doc: 1 rifle, 1 fusil, 1 sable, 2 sacos de balas, 2 frascos de pólvora, aparejos de mochilas, 1 silla completa con freno, 1 freno, 5 caballos (signed Vallejo)
