# (Docts en francés) THE CAMBUSTON QUICKSILVER-MINE CASE: forwards a claim from the Frenchman Cambuston re his denouncemen

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n317–318** · Sav p. 317 · 1848-05-18 · Savage abstract · 

(Docts en francés) THE CAMBUSTON QUICKSILVER-MINE CASE: forwards a claim from the Frenchman Cambuston re his denouncement (12 Feb 1848) not being published at San José until the 18th; asks the decree apply from date of publication at Monterey, giving it retrospective effect (rejected in all civilized countries); remits: (1) copy of the denouncement by the denouncer Cambuston, Sonél, Chabolla y Sainsevain; (2) copy of the pregones; (3) certificate of the pueblo alcalde; (4) copy of Cambuston's 15 May 1848 letter — the denouncement concerns an azogue (quicksilver/mercury) mine near San Juan Bautista, 1,000 varas S of Pedro Chabolla's house & ~500 from the Camino Real; the 12 Feb 1848 Monterey decree suspending mine-denouncements (that the already-possessed deal with the U.S. govt) was published at San José 18 Feb; the denouncement was made 13 Feb; per the decree they wished to annul Chabolla's rights & suspend the mine works; the docs do not say whether the claim took effect — ⭐ an early (pre-gold-rush) quicksilver-mining denouncement, cf. New Almadén context
