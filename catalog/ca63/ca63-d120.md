# Frauds by James D. Hutton, late surveyor of the district: requests a special tribunal to try Hutton for obtaining money 

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n45–46** · Sav p. 45 · 1848-01-12 · Savage abstract · 

Frauds by James D. Hutton, late surveyor of the district: requests a special tribunal to try Hutton for obtaining money under false pretenses & violation of official duties, certifying lots surveyed to contain 500 acres each when re-surveyed by present official surveyor C.S. Lyman found to contain only 300–350 acres; Hutton said to be now surveying in Sonoma district, attempting to get out of the alcalde's jurisdiction; on 31 Dec a meeting of the late Junta of the pueblo (Wm. Fisher, Salvador Castro, Chas. White, José Noriega, Antº Suñol & Dolores Pacheco) w/ late Alcalde John Burton & present alcalde considered the matter, all but Suñol & Pacheco (who did not dissent) pronounced the acts of Hutton a fraud upon the community; reports to Gov. to appoint the special court to try the case
