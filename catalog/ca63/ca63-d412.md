# Remits copy of a complaint from the Frenchman Clément Panaud whom the founding alcalde of San Juan, on a false accusatio

**C-A 63 (Miscellaneous / Unbound Documents), scan n291–292** · Sav p. 291 · 1847-06-27 · Savage abstract · 

Remits copy of a complaint from the Frenchman Clément Panaud whom the founding alcalde of San Juan, on a false accusation, jailed & marched to Monterey as a criminal; cites the case of Pedro Richard, French gunsmith, from whom the officer Maddox refused to give receipts for arms seized, imprisoned & maltreated; the said Frenchman wrongly accused of giving arms to the enemy (there being no enemy), when as a good Frenchman he strictly observed the neutrality — with English translation of Panaud's 16 Jun 1847 letter re being jailed on the false charge of possessing 'un baril de brandy y dos jarros de fierro'
