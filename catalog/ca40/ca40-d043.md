# The full Open-Door defense. Quoting the Comisº Genⁿ's 7-Dec reprimand (for letting the brig 'Anglo-Am. Nº 8' sell 'break

**C-A 40 (Departmental State Papers, Benicia (Commissary & Treasury)), scan n42–n47** · Sav p. 43–48 · tomo p. 284–295 · 1826-07-11 · Savage abstract · 

The full Open-Door defense. Quoting the Comisº Genⁿ's 7-Dec reprimand (for letting the brig 'Anglo-Am. Nº 8' sell 'breaking the laws that absolutely prohibit this') and the Minister of Hacienda's charge, Herrera argues foreign commerce is 'the only resource the National Treasury has in this territory,' that the missions (its chief customers) depend on coastal foreign trade to buy necessities & move their *frutos*, that rigid legal observance is impossible given distance/established practice/lack of transport — yet under threat of liability he suspends foreign trade pending the Supreme Govt's ruling & circulates the ban to the 3 habilitados (SF/SB/SD). Triggered by the Amⁿ ship *Courier*
