# Codified *Reglamento* for foreign-ship traffic: a year's experience showed the harm of letting ships trade 'at discretio

**C-A 40 (Departmental State Papers, Benicia (Commissary & Treasury)), scan n82–n84** · Sav p. 83–85 · tomo p. 634+ · 1827-01-22 · Savage abstract · 

Codified *Reglamento* for foreign-ship traffic: a year's experience showed the harm of letting ships trade 'at discretion' from where they anchor on their own word; now every captain/supercargo shown the tariff, must present a manifest (*nota*) of goods to land (checked for quantity/quality), trade under guard vigilance (embargo penalty for smuggling), and on departure reconcile the remainder so duties fall on what was sold — the administrative machinery of managing tolerated foreign trade
