# Held personally liable by the Min. de Hacienda (via Comisº Genⁿ de Occidente) for having let foreign ships trade 'agains

**C-A 40 (Departmental State Papers, Benicia (Commissary & Treasury)), scan n16** · Sav p. 17 · tomo p. 169–170 · 1826-06-28 · Savage abstract · 

Held personally liable by the Min. de Hacienda (via Comisº Genⁿ de Occidente) for having let foreign ships trade 'against the laws,' Herrera orders the habilitados to bar any arriving foreign vessel from loading/unloading any goods until the Comandante Genⁿ rules (unsigned draft) — *the Open-Door tension: foreign commerce illegal-but-indispensable*
