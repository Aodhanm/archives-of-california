# The presidial self-financing model laid bare: can't pay militia without supreme order; defense-works & garrison costs ex

**C-A 40 (Departmental State Papers, Benicia (Commissary & Treasury)), scan n56–n60** · Sav p. 57–61 · tomo p. 345–354 · 1826-10-14 · Savage abstract · 

The presidial self-financing model laid bare: can't pay militia without supreme order; defense-works & garrison costs exceed San Diego's income; California's income 'generally doesn't cover its expenses' → each presidio sustains itself from its own maritime duties + eventual *alcabala* + the missions' tithes; Monterey too runs a deficit yet pays *libranzas* 'in favor of foreigners'; reconciles $1,500+ owed to William Hartnell & 135 fanegas wheat for an SF launch; no faculty to take loans, least of all from foreigners; when funds fall short, scarcity shared proportionally & troops preferred over officers
