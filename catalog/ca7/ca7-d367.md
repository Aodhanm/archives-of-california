# Baja California pearl-fishery (*buceo de perlas*) report (R. Tribunal de Cuentas, 28 May / 21 Sep 1795): remit to San Bl

**C-A 7 (Provincial State Papers), scan n264–n270** · Sav p. (46)–(52) · tomo p. xiii.106–116 · 1795-10-24 · Savage abstract · 

Baja California pearl-fishery (*buceo de perlas*) report (R. Tribunal de Cuentas, 28 May / 21 Sep 1795): remit to San Blas the 23 oz 10 adarmes of pearls at Loreto for the royal *quinto*; Borica's plan to resume the fishery (1-yr quinto exemption); *armadores* Martínez, Valdés, Mesa, Munguía of Mulegé (1791–93); 5 canoes in 1794; the fishery's conduct (armador + 20–30 divers; shell/pearl shares); Indian-diver protections under the Laws of the Indies (libs. 4 tít. 25; freedom, no death-penalty coercion); + a comparative essay on world pearling (Ceylon, Persian Gulf/Bahrain, Japan via Goa/Lisbon, Bengal, Madagascar) & the pearl 'La Peregrina' — Bourbon political-economy applied to Cal. Ratified Bonilla 26 Nov
