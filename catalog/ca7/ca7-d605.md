# The fullest account of the English whaler *Resolución* / Capt. John Locke (late Aug 1795): coastal sighting off San Migu

**C-A 7 (Provincial State Papers), scan n478–n483** · Sav p. (259)–(264) · tomo p. xiii.585–590 · 1795-10-01 · Savage abstract · 

The fullest account of the English whaler *Resolución* / Capt. John Locke (late Aug 1795): coastal sighting off San Miguel; signal-fires & shots; the escort *pedrero* + horse tumble into the estero (horse killed, gun unusable); ship to the Ensenada de Todos Santos; boarding party ashore (captain + English interpreter + 2 Black crew); passport read — frigate *Resolución*, merchant, left London 1 Nov 1793, chartered by the British Crown, Capt. Juan (John) Locke, 2 officers, crew of 50 + 1 widow, whaling (300 barrels sperm & oil), bound London via Acapulco; Missions Santo Tomás & San Miguel provisioned it (3 cattle, 110 arrobas flour, 15 fan. maize…) against a bill in Cádiz/London → folds into E25
