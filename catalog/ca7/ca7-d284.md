# General foreign-ship EXCLUSION order, synthesizing the whole policy (verified scale 1; see E16): no foreign ships in Ame

**C-A 7 (Provincial State Papers), scan n189–n190** · Sav p. 185–186 · tomo p. 485–486 · 1794-11-12 · Savage abstract · 

General foreign-ship EXCLUSION order, synthesizing the whole policy (verified scale 1; see E16): no foreign ships in American ports — expel any arrival 'sin pretexto ni excusa,' no one 'por privilegiada q[ue] sea' to trade 'directa ó indirectamente,' nor even supply victuals; only in the remote case of urgent necessity give 'los precisos auxilios que exige la Hospitalidad, y pida el Derecho de Gentes,' with precautions 'á q[ue] no tomen conocimto del país'; and names Vancouver & Puget (in port w/ the *Descubierta* & *Chatham*) — already given full aid at Monterey, so any request at other ports 'deberá ser destituida,' made to understand the orders so they retire
