**C-A 12 (Prov. St. Pap. XIX), scan n269–n271** · tomo 718–720 · 1809-02-16 San Francisco · Savage abstract ·

The foundational Russian-incursion intelligence report: on 14 Feb two Anglo-Americans presented themselves among the christian Indians from the other side of the port, saying they deserted a Russian frigate anchored at Bodega — one of the four men the 'Genl ruso D.n Nicolás Resanow' had held as exiled prisoners on the island facing the presidio (1806); three armed Russians appeared at the padres' house at 12¼. Interrogation yields: the frigate is 'The-Coniach' [Kad'iak], her commander 'Goosebfh' [Ivan Kuskov], 40 Russians and 150 Indians incl. 20 women from Unalaska/Kodiak; the canoe-hunters withdrew NOT by the port but landing at the ensenada de los Huymenes, portaging over the hills to Punta de Bonetes; heavy Russian/American trade with the local naturales, who already carry their jackets, shirts and cloths. Coast clear from Punta de Bonetes to Punta de Reyes at writing; an Unalaska Indian sent [to Monterey] per the Gov.'s order. Published: Mathes (1990) Doc 3.
