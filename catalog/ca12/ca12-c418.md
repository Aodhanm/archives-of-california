# ⭐⭐ "Expedición militar — Combate con los indios": 22 Oct, 10 SF-presidio soldiers (mission launch) joined Sgt. Francisco

**C-A 12 (Prov. St. Pap. XIX), scan n351–n355** · tomo 1153–69 · 1813-10-31 San Francisco · Savage abstract · 

⭐⭐ "Expedición militar — Combate con los indios": 22 Oct, 10 SF-presidio soldiers (mission launch) joined Sgt. Francisco Soto (from Mission San José; 2 soldiers + 100 Indian auxiliaries) to recover San José-mission cimarrones; night river navigation; reached a large, wooded, swampy island ranchería at dawn the 27th. Forewarned, the cimarrones had massed 4 rancherías (~1,000 armed Indians), posted sentinels, sent away women/children, and put up "porfiada resistencia," attacking from all sides. ~3-hour fight, driven off "á vivo fuego"; the swamp (water knee-deep) let them swim to another Tulares island & escape — none captured but "bien escarmentados." The Indians had taunted that "soldiers are worth nothing on foot, only on horseback," then learned otherwise. Spanish loss: one auxiliary, "Julio," killed + one lightly wounded; heavy enemy loss. Soto praises the auxiliaries' obedience & valor. [→ military-history register.]
