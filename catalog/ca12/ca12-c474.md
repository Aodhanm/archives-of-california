# Dictamen prohibiting commerce with English ships re the English schooner *Columbia* at Monterey & the North West Company

**C-A 12 (Prov. St. Pap. XIX), scan n397–n399** · tomo 1374–87 · 1815-07-08 México · Savage abstract · ⭐⭐

Dictamen prohibiting commerce with English ships re the English schooner *Columbia* at Monterey & the North West Company of Canada agents' protest: the Company holds an establishment on the Columbia at 46° — founded by Anglo-Americans ~8 years ago, then seized by the English Company [Astoria → Fort George], garrisoned, corresponding with Asia; agents sought to land European goods (Solá might allow it for lack of memorias); the schooner left 10 deserters; partner [McTavish] stayed at Monterey with a carpenter to provision the post, waiting at Bodega while writing London; suspicion they aim to seize the territory under pretext of the beaver trade; orders to watch Fort George, prevent commerce/contraband, provision only per hospitality. (Asesor 4 Jul; Calleja signs 8 Jul 1815.)
