# ⭐⭐ Measures to cut off commerce with American ships. An American two-masted ship anchored near Mission San Luis [Obispo]

**C-A 12 (Prov. St. Pap. XIX), scan n115–n116** · tomo 284–287 · 1806-02-08 Monterey · verbatim transcription · 

⭐⭐ Measures to cut off commerce with American ships. An American two-masted ship anchored near Mission San Luis [Obispo] on pretext of wood/water; Arrillaga: the pretext hides commercial (or "más siniestros") intent — "if our side did not deal with them, they would abandon our coasts." Orders guards (sgt/cabo + 4 soldiers) at landing points, day & night, deny all provisions/water/wood, seize the foreigners if possible (citing Borica's earlier circular of the law of the Indies).
