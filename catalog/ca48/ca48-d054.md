# California as a penal colony (objection) + ruined presidios: answers the supreme order to send *presidio*-convicts to Al

**C-A 48 (Departmental Records), scan n37–n39** · Sav p.  · tomo p. p.213–225 · 1829-09-18 · Savage abstract · 

California as a penal colony (objection) + ruined presidios: answers the supreme order to send *presidio*-convicts to Alta Calif. — would accept exiles/deserters (useful as vecinos/soldiers) but hardened criminals would bring 'total corrupción de costumbres'; 'En la Alta California no hay ningún preso[/presidio]' — the 4 presidios are ruined adobe-hut squares, no moat/palisade/walls/batteries/artillery/sentinels/water; the only jail 'defectuosa é insegura'; too few troops; no money even for the 'real y medio' daily to feed a convict; asks the supreme govt to modify the order & send back the convicts already shipped
