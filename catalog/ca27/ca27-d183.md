# Jedediah Smith's own letter: he and his men are 'Americanos que vamos en persecución del Río de Columbia'; was at Missio

**C-A 27 (Departmental State Papers), scan n179–n181** · Sav p. II:17 · tomo p. 33–34 · 1827-05-19 · verbatim transcription · 

Jedediah Smith's own letter: he and his men are 'Americanos que vamos en persecución del Río de Columbia'; was at Mission San Gabriel last January, got a passport to San Diego, but deep Sierra snow blocked his crossing to the other side; camped among friendly Indians awaiting the thaw; lost horses; without clothing, subsisting on 'carne cimarrona'; signs 'conocido extranjero pero verdadero amigo y su hermano cristiano.' Savage's note: the original (p.38) shows it was addressed to Fr. Narciso Durán (Mission San José)
