# On encounters with the Indians — a rich borderlands + Indian-affairs dossier: Ewing Young ('Joaquín Young') and his 22-m

**C-A 27 (Departmental State Papers), scan n299–n303** · Sav p. II:135 · tomo p. 445, 136–139, 442–444 · 1830-07-15 · verbatim transcription · 

On encounters with the Indians — a rich borderlands + Indian-affairs dossier: Ewing Young ('Joaquín Young') and his 22-man party arrive at Mission San José (from the Valle de San Luis, a day from New Mexico), to buy horses & trap beaver; shows two English passports differing only in date (1828 & 1829) [= Clay passports, docs 250/288] + one sealed at the Mexican Legation, Washington, 20 Mar 1828; Young's Americans join Mission San José's Indian alcalde Francisco Ximénez, who raised a 450-man gentile-auxiliary army (Cosumnes/Ochejamenes etc.) to recover fugitive neophytes; the fugitive captain Huyumecsepa refuses to return them, saying at the mission 'solo les daban muchos azotes' (⭐ an Indian indictment of mission floggings); a 3-hour firefight; ⭐⭐⭐ intelligence that Estanislao, pardoned cabecilla of the prior year's (1829) neophyte revolt, is out in the Tulares with 100 horses under a padre's license, and on the San Joaquín rancherías' horse herds (Moquelumnes, the 'capitán general' Tinay)
