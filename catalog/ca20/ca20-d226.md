# The Temecula/San Luis Rey neophyte land-conspiracy — *'Causa criminal contra Indios de San Luis Rey, acusados de tumultu

**C-A 20 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n193–n199** · Sav p. 194–200 / 380–414 · tomo p. **1835 (events Apr; causa closed 5 Nov)** · San Luis Rey / Temecula → Monterey (Gutiérrez, Com. Gen.) · Savage abstract · 

The Temecula/San Luis Rey neophyte land-conspiracy — *'Causa criminal contra Indios de San Luis Rey, acusados de tumultuarios'* (✅ pinned & CORRECTED 2026-07-02, scans n193–199). The grievance is a LAND GRANT, not cattle-seizure (earlier catalog reading corrected): the ex-mission neophytes held a *junta* at the Temascal (11 Apr 1835, ~3 PM) opposing the grant of the Rancho de Temecula (+ more land) to Coronel José Antonio Estudillo, arguing the land is 'propiedad de todos' and that no authority holds dominion over those ranchos — vowing that if the territorial government gave Temecula to Estudillo 'se armarían' and would summon the gentiles de la Sierra. Principal: Julián Murillo; co-accused José Miguel & Juan Nepomuceno. Informers: neophytes Julián & Marcial → Capt. Pablo de la Portilla → Gov. Figueroa (16 Apr). Fiscal Subtte Patricio Estrada (escribano José de Jesús González); declarations from 16 Apr, interrogatory 17 Apr. Outcome: Figueroa freed the accused; Gutiérrez archived the sumaria (Monterey, 5 Nov 1835) — no punishment. A secularization-era indigenous land-rights resistance — pairs directly with Doc 242 (Vázquez del Mercado's 'propietarios' argument). → [[indian-raiding-article]], [[californio-article]], mission-critique
