# The volume's tail — a heavily *interleaved* run of fiscal/postal/muster records across many years (NOT chronological): ⭐

**C-A 20 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n401–n540** · Sav p. 401–540 / — · tomo p. 1817–1845 (interleaved) · Mont / SB / SD / SJ / Méx · Savage abstract · 

The volume's tail — a heavily *interleaved* run of fiscal/postal/muster records across many years (NOT chronological): ⭐⭐ Nicolás Gutiérrez's private 1836 letter to Gov. Chico on the mounting discontent (Cosme Peña agitating the alcaldes/ayuntamientos; the SB unrest over the Padre Presidente's departure) — the political tension feeding the 1836 Alvarado revolution; ⭐ 1817–1824 Santa Bárbara company musters + *hojas de servicio* (de la Guerra, Maitorena, Carlos Carrillo, Gabriel Moraga, young Antonio de la Guerra); ⭐ 1824–1827 postal/*correos* administration (Herrera; Joaquín Estudillo → SF postmaster 1827; Suñol at San José; Domingo Carrillo at SD); interleaved Spanish-era mission-fiscal docs (the 1772 Reglamento, the Loreto Temporalidades/situados, the Baja-Cal $16,450 military budget); 1832–33 artillery musters (43-man 5th artillery co.) + Figueroa correspondence; 1844–45 paymaster (Rafael Sánchez) accounts of the Batallón Permanente. ⚠ The 1840 Graham affair & 1841 Fort Ross sale were NOT caught in the tail gap-sample — a targeted re-read may be worthwhile if those events are needed.
