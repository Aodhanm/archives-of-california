# divorce/separation suit — Encarnación Alcívar vs. her husband Sgt Andrés Cervantes (artillery): dagger-threat, drunken a

**C-A 20 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n39–45** · Sav p. 40–46 · tomo p. 703–800 · 1834-09 → 1835-01 · Savage abstract · 

divorce/separation suit — Encarnación Alcívar vs. her husband Sgt Andrés Cervantes (artillery): dagger-threat, drunken abuse, no support. A conciliation act (arbiters *hombres buenos*: Zamorano for her, W. Hartnell for him) failed — she refused to reconcile; pregnant, she sought a *casa de depósito* at her mother's house + costs on Cervantes. Castillo Negrete's opinion: true divorce belongs to the ecclesiastical judge (the missionary-vicar, for want of a military chaplain), but secular courts may penalize a *'marido cruel'*; orders the couple to reunite, Cervantes to post a peace bond + up to a month's arrest for the prohibited dagger.
