# the 1834 San Gabriel 'conspiracy' investigation — Figueroa (on 3 letters from Ángel Ramírez the customs adm., Fr. Blas O

**C-A 20 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n23–39** · Sav p. 24–40 · tomo p. 635–701 · 1834-05-26 → 10-02 · Savage abstract · 

the 1834 San Gabriel 'conspiracy' investigation — Figueroa (on 3 letters from Ángel Ramírez the customs adm., Fr. Blas Ordaz, & Antonio Mª Lugo) has Gutiérrez investigate Capt. José de la Guerra, Fr. Narciso Durán (Padre Presidente), Fr. Tomás Estenaga, Sgt José Antⁿ Pico — accused of favoring a central over the federal government, circulating a secretly-signed petition, and diverting San Gabriel mission money. ⭐ The 'petition' proved a legit 1829 cattle contract (San Gabriel→de la Guerra, Rancho de Simí; $1,251 receipt); the money = legitimate mission accounting ($1,000 to W. Hartnell, schooling costs); Chapman recanted. OUTCOME: fiscal Gutiérrez dismisses the case (2 Oct 1834); de la Guerra & Durán exonerated, restored to 'la buena opinión y fama que siempre han tenido' — *expanded below*
