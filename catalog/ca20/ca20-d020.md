# self-defense murder *sumaria* — soldier Joaquín Alfaro (Hidalgo battalion) killed Encarnación Hernández in a knife-fight

**C-A 20 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n9–18** · Sav p. 10–19 · tomo p. 277–441 · 1834-06-10 → 07 · Savage abstract · 

self-defense murder *sumaria* — soldier Joaquín Alfaro (Hidalgo battalion) killed Encarnación Hernández in a knife-fight; Alfaro's account: Hernández demanded a debt, attacked first with knife then bayonet (wounding Alfaro's face & thigh); ⭐ surgeon Manuel Alba's forensic autopsy (a 12-line × 70-deep stab through heart/lung); Gertrudis Garibay (who'd had relations with both men) implicated then referred to civil authority; defender Alférez Ignacio del Valle; Alfaro took church sanctuary at Mission San Carlos (Fr. Suárez del Real); case truncated in Savage's copy ('trunca aquí') — *expanded below*
