# ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Sola on the Quintana murder's motives — the iron whip and the secret punishment inquiry (Apr 1816)

**C-A 25 (Provincial Records (Tomo IX)), scans n390–n391** · Sav pp. 387–388 · tomo leaves 138–139 · orig pp. 470–76+ · Mar–Apr 1816 · Savage abstract (near-verbatim) ·

Sola reports to the viceroy why the Santa Cruz Indians killed Fr. Andrés Quintana (Oct 1812): the padre "había mandado hacer una cuarta de hierro p.a azotarles"; Lino, son of Andrés, was heavily punished, and those flogged with the iron whip "se vieron á punto de morirse"; the fiscal did not justify the punishment, and Sola — "conociendo ser de su obligación evitar estas barbaridades" — has made secret inquiries into the punishments used across the 19 missions, finding the ministers generally affectionate but some using a two-strand discipline for public sins. Same leaf: the sentences (200 lashes + 10/6 years) and the March 5 report that the troops and their families have gone unpaid for six years. Direct evidentiary chain with the Horra expediente (ca22-d2001) on the Indian-treatment question.
