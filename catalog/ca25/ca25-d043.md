# ⭐⭐⭐ Borrador register, leaf 131 ([1813]) — the Flora's prize towed to Santa Bárbara

**C-A 25 (Provincial Records (Tomo IX)), scan n383** · Sav p. 380 · tomo leaf 131 · orig pp. 416–25 · Sept–Oct [1813] · Savage abstract (register) ·

The October entries close the *Mercury* arc: Nicolás Noé of the *Flora* "apresó una fragata por andar en corso y remolcándola hasta S.ta Bárbara" (the American otter ship *Mercury*, taken June 1813 — Savage's register renders the smuggling seizure as privateering); the *Catalina* under [Tagle/Unanue?] is to carry "Americano Jorge" (Capt. George W. Eayrs) and his people to San Blas; and the *Flora* ships 3 Russian deserters to San Blas. September's items receive the Cádiz decrees — including the 6 Aug 1811 extinction of señorío privileges — and the wartime commerce contribution. Bancroft's ix.131 cites (HoC II:268, 418) land here.
