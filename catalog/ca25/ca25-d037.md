# Arrillaga to the Viceroy, 28 June 1810 — Moraga's campaign against the Sespesuya ranchería (Suisun)

**C-A 25 (Provincial Records (Tomo IX)), scan n374–n375** · Sav p. 371–372 · tomo leaf 122–123 · orig pp. 367–71 · 1810-06-28 · Savage abstract ·

"Sobre encuentro y campaña con gentiles." Arrillaga's full report to the viceroy on Alférez Gabriel Moraga's May 1810 expedition against the Sespesuya ranchería across the estero from San Francisco (the Suisun fight): 16 cristianos killed during three years of nominal peace; no launches — the troop crossed the strait by relays in a single small boat; 18 wounded prisoners taken and released as sure to die; the survivors' last stand in three yacales, two cabos and two soldiers wounded and none of the troop killed; the third yacal fired, its defenders dying in the flames rather than surrendering; ~120 gentiles reckoned engaged. Moraga recommended for teniente, the wounded cabos for sergeant, the soldiers for an escudo de ventajas.

**Bancroft:** Hist. Cal. II:90–91 (the Suisun narrative), footnote 21: "June 28, 1810, Arrillaga's report to viceroy. Prov. Rec., MS., ix. 122-3." Bancroft's "122-3" = the handwritten per-tomo leaf numbers on these two scans (122, 123), not the original tomo pages (367–71) — the two systems coexist on every leaf of this volume (see errata).
