# The Reyes vs. Capt. Noriega jurisdiction causa (SB): a complaint by Antonio Reyes against Capt. José de la Guerra y Nori

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n394–n399** · Sav p. 389–394 · tomo p. 33–42 · 1833-04-30 · Savage abstract · 

The Reyes vs. Capt. Noriega jurisdiction causa (SB): a complaint by Antonio Reyes against Capt. José de la Guerra y Noriega raises whether a military commander may judge civilians where there is no ayuntamiento. Gómez's dictamen — plus a candid private letter — holds that where there are no constitutional alcaldes the military commander exercises 1st-instance justice, but ⚠️ declares Santa Bárbara's 'Alcalde Constitucional' illegally established (SB lacked the population/elements; the Diputación gave him only *Juez Conciliador* standing), and frets that saying so 'abriría heridas políticas que aún no están cicatrizadas' (he has been called 'enemigo del territorio' for such opinions). A vivid source on the contested legitimacy of civil vs. military authority in 1833 California. + an out-of-order 1821 SB muster (de la Guerra's company, 69 men) and a July 1833 Monterey muster. Orig. pp.33–42.
