# Causa vs. Luis, a Santa Clara neófito, for killing Atanasio (another SC neophyte). A long case (1830–1833, ~114 pp. of d

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n321–n324** · Sav p. 316–319 · tomo p. 2–127 · 1833-02-25 · verbatim transcription · 

Causa vs. Luis, a Santa Clara neófito, for killing Atanasio (another SC neophyte). A long case (1830–1833, ~114 pp. of declarations; several Indians — León, Marcos, Lucas — held on suspicion); fiscals Berreyesa then Sánchez; defender Alf. M. G. Vallejo. Luis petitioned Gov. Figueroa (arrived 1833) over ~2 years' imprisonment for a calumny. ⭐⭐ Asesor Gómez (13 Oct 1831): the evidence is weak — absolve Luis; and open a sumaria against fiscal sargto Berreyesa for falsifying the witness declarations (Vicente & Tiliburo[?]). The case stalled during the 1831–32 political turmoil (Victoria's overthrow). Gov. Figueroa (16 Feb 1833) freed Luis, restored to his mission after ~2.5 years' wrongful imprisonment. A miscarriage-of-justice / fiscal-falsification case spanning the Victoria→Figueroa transition. Orig. pp.2–108, 116–127.
