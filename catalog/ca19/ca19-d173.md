# Causa vs. Juan Pablo, a Santa Bárbara neófito, for inducing ~10 fellow neophytes to make a 'representación violenta' aga

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n317–n319** · Sav p. 312–314 · tomo p. 779–800 · 1831-10-13 · verbatim transcription · 

Causa vs. Juan Pablo, a Santa Bárbara neófito, for inducing ~10 fellow neophytes to make a 'representación violenta' against their Padre Ministro — complaining the Padre had sold / was selling the mission's cattle, the roof-tiles of the mission houses, the mules, and the beach house (asset-stripping; recruited via Francisco Soya[?], Hilario the blacksmith, et al.). They petitioned alférez Domingo Carrillo. Fiscal sought 1 yr public works. ⭐⭐ Asesor Gómez sides with the neophytes: free Juan Pablo, and instead open an investigation into the Padre, who — if the complaint proves true — is to be told 'no estar facultado p[ar]a vender las fincas de la misión, y mucho menos p[ar]a acabar los alimentos y vestuario á los neófitos' when there are esquilmos to cover it. (End not present.) A striking mission-asset-stripping / indigenous-protest case on the eve of secularization (pairs with Expanded Entry E). Orig. pp.779–800.
