# Service record of Romualdo Pacheco (military engineer; father of the future Gov. Romualdo Pacheco): cadet 1 yr 6 mo, tot

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n7–n9** · Sav p. 7–9 · tomo p. 290–292 · 1829-07-31 · Savage abstract · 

Service record of Romualdo Pacheco (military engineer; father of the future Gov. Romualdo Pacheco): cadet 1 yr 6 mo, total 13 yrs incl. campaign credits. Two Colorado-River expeditions (1825 Nov & 1826 May): the first w/ only 4 soldiers — reconnoitered the river, found a favorable road, made peace with hostile tribes; the second w/ 25 dragoons (his troop nearly abandoned him before 'la inmensa muchedumbre de indios'); built a bridge. April 1828 reconnaissance of the whole port of San Francisco & its rivers (chose a fort site by Supreme-Govt order). ⭐⭐ 18 Oct 1828: as adjutant of Monterey, helped suppress 'la sublevación que hicieron los soldados de la Compañía de Caballería de Monterey' ('el sostén de la plaza vulnerada por la tropa') — the 1828 Monterey cavalry revolt, precursor to the 1829 Solís revolt. Then took the Santa Bárbara command (31 Dec 1828) under Cmdt.-Gen. Echeandía. Orig. pp.290–292.
