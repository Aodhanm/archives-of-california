# Measure to check the Russians of Ross/Bodega — the grant of 'Santa Rosa' as a barrier. Echeandía, holding it 'de suma im

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n64–n65** · Sav p. 64–65 · tomo p. 166–167 · 1831-01-08 · verbatim transcription · 

Measure to check the Russians of Ross/Bodega — the grant of 'Santa Rosa' as a barrier. Echeandía, holding it 'de suma importancia á la seguridad é integridad del territorio nacional,' resolves to plant a settlement on the land between Mission San Rafael and the Russian establishment of the puerto de Bodega, 'que sirva de barrera' against foreign incursion 'para posesionarse de él, como lo han hecho del que ocupan' (i.e., as the Russians did at Bodega) — prompted by the San Rafael missionary's fear (after a Russian reconnaissance the prior year) and to draw in the gentile Indians. He grants the tract known as Santa Rosa to the petitioner D. R. Gómez[?] (with powers for agriculture & stock-raising) under Art. 12 of the 18 Aug 1824 colonization decree, provisional pending the Diputación. (Borrador.) The Mexican continuation of Solá's 1818 San Rafael barrier strategy vs. Fort Ross. Orig. pp.166–167.
