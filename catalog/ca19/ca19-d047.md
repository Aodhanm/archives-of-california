# Inquiry into Indians of San José & Santa Clara killed on a punitive expedition. A party under Alférez [Gonzalo?] de Vall

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n45** · Sav p. 45 · tomo p. 518–541 · 1829-10-31 · verbatim transcription · 

Inquiry into Indians of San José & Santa Clara killed on a punitive expedition. A party under Alférez [Gonzalo?] de Vallejo (⚠️ the first name as written resembles 'Gonzalo,' but in context — SF company alférez 1º, 1829 — this is almost certainly Mariano Guadalupe Vallejo) pursuing Christian Indians of San José & Santa Clara killed & robbed Indians incl. 3 defenseless old women; the fiscal finds only one Indian was shot (not lanced) and that a Monterey soldier ([Joaquín?] Alvarado) killed an elderly gentile woman — 'aunque fuese en el acto de la guerra, no le es permitido á ninguna persona [atacar] á los del sexo femenino' — recommending 5 extra years on the Baja Cal. frontier. ⚠️ *I'm inferring* this is the atrocity inquiry following M. G. Vallejo's May–June 1829 campaign against Estanislao (an SJ-mission neophyte) — dates/place/perpetrator fit, but verify against the Estanislao record before asserting. Orig. pp.518–541.
