# Salary-arrears statement: as of 30 Jun 1834, total owed this year to presidial companies + military/civil salaried = $52

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n465** · Sav p. 459 · tomo p. 15+ · 1834-09-08 · Savage abstract · 

Salary-arrears statement: as of 30 Jun 1834, total owed this year to presidial companies + military/civil salaried = $52,835; total accrued $134,072; received $80,657; arrears mostly from 1833, some 1832, a few back to 1817–1827. Notes the office lacks archives/books/documents to compile a complete account. Includes a ⭐ companion roster (n460–n466) naming the whole unattached/retired officer corps + civil employees owed salary — Figueroa, Nicolás Gutiérrez, Juan Antonio Muñoz, Ignacio Martínez, Francisco Ma. Ruiz, Mariano Estrada, José de la Guerra y Noriega, Antonio del Valle, José Ma. Ramírez, Juan José Rocha, Bernardo Navarrete, José Peña, et al. A stark presidio-decay / fiscal-collapse datapoint + 1834 officer prosopography. Orig. pp.15, 329–30.
