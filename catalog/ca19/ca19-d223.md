# The penal-colony convict register (→ Expanded Entry G): a compiled dossier of convict-release petitions 'por [las cuales

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n407–n412** · Sav p. 402–407 · tomo p. 183–306 · 1833–35 · Savage abstract · 

The penal-colony convict register (→ Expanded Entry G): a compiled dossier of convict-release petitions 'por [las cuales] aparece que este territorio era colonia penal de México' — ~25+ named presidiarios with sentence dates & 1833–35 liberty decrees (Moreno, Roso, Trinidad Cruz, Rangel, Barreto, Vásquez, M. Sánchez, J.A. López, Badillo, Olvera, Quiñones, Vargas, Sandoval, Arcena, Gallardo, Mata, Domingo Hernández, Francisco Padilla — the one *denied* release & recommended for Veracruz hard labor — et al.), plus the standing order that a convict leaving his assigned place is 'irremisiblemente pasado por las armas.' A systematic prosopographical source on California-as-penal-colony. Orig. pp.183–306.
