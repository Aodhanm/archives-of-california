# Robbery of the Mission San Carlos almacén (storehouse; reported 13 May 1831 by Padre Fr. Ramón Abella to Gov. Victoria).

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n245–n248** · Sav p. 240–243 · tomo p. 287–357 · 1831-05-28 · verbatim transcription · 

Robbery of the Mission San Carlos almacén (storehouse; reported 13 May 1831 by Padre Fr. Ramón Abella to Gov. Victoria). Pliego investigated the tendajos for the stolen goods; culprits: presidiario [Simón/José] Aguilar, the civilian Eduardo Lagarra[?], and Andrés (San Carlos neophyte); a neófito Rafael held first (a piece of cotín on him). The fiscal sought the death penalty ('pasados por las armas') for Aguilar & Lagarra; the 13-yr-old accomplice → 200 lashes. Asesor Gómez: Aguilar merits 'el último suplicio' as a 'ladrón famoso' (prior robbery conviction). ⭐⭐ Sentence: Aguilar & Lagarra 'pasados por las armas' (SHOT) — executed 28 May 1831 in the fiscal's presence; the neophyte Andrés (who held the almacén key) made to watch, then 100 lashes + a corma + 6 months' forced mission labor; 4 other Indians freed with a warning. Gov. Victoria confirmed (26 May). Orig. pp.287–357.
