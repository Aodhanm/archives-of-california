# The Charles Laing contraband investigation. Laing, jailed & accused of smuggling; Echeandía (12 Feb) had Tte. Juan Mª Ib

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n11–n14** · Sav p. 11–14 · tomo p. 431–461 · 1829-02-26 · verbatim transcription · 

The Charles Laing contraband investigation. Laing, jailed & accused of smuggling; Echeandía (12 Feb) had Tte. Juan Mª Ibarra depose the foreigners — 2 Americans + 2 Kanakas (Hawaiians) who landed at San Quintín (Baja Cal.). Their ship was the frigate *Rambler* (out of Nantucket); Laing (a U.S. native & navigator) had come from Oahu on the brig *Alabama* to settle in Baja or Alta Cal. by permission, was put off into the launch after a falling-out at Isla Redonda, survived on sea-birds & fish, traded the soldiers' game for silver; effects landed at the ensenada de Todos Santos; sailor Winslow Lewis, Kanakas Espen/Carena[?]. Echeandía's decree (26 Feb 1829): return Laing's víveres/clothes/papers, hold the powder/balls/arms in deposit, the rest in the Aduana; the launch to the port commander; copy to the Tribunal de circuito for sentence. Orig. pp.431–461.
