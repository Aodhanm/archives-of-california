# Sentence in the San Diego Indian-uprising conspiracy. A planned general uprising of the SD-area Indians (reported by Tom

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n375–n378** · Sav p. 370–373 · tomo p. 314–528 · 1833-11-22 · verbatim transcription · 

Sentence in the San Diego Indian-uprising conspiracy. A planned general uprising of the SD-area Indians (reported by Tomás Olivera from Rancho Tecate): to gather the convened Indians, assault the San Diego presidio on the 20th, 'acabar con los blancos p[ar]a ser independientes, y gobernarse ellos mismos,' the same intended for the missions up to San Gabriel (emissaries carried the convocatoria). Accused: José Antonio Molina (emissary), Tomás Tesochi, Ramón, Andrés, Gregorio, Francisco Roman, Domingo. Investigated by Argüello, continued by fiscal Nicolás Gutiérrez under Figueroa/Gómez → consejo de guerra (pres. Capt. Juan Antonio Muñoz; vocales Zamorano, González, Navarrete, Peña; Alf. Francisco Pacheco). Sentence: Molina & Tesochi → 2 yrs public works; Roman → 1 yr; Gregorio & another → 1 yr; Domingo → freed — penalties attenuated for 'la gran ignorancia de los acusados' & their not executing the plot; ⭐⭐ the Asesor acknowledged 'la probabilidad de los malos tratamientos de q[u]e se quejan, y alegan por motivos de su asonada' (the likely mistreatment cited as the uprising's motive). Figueroa confirmed 22 Nov 1833. A significant indigenous-resistance case with an explicit independence / self-government aim and the authorities conceding Indian grievances. Orig. pp.314–528.
