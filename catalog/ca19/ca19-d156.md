# Causa criminal vs. José Mariano Duarte, Alcalde of San José, for getting the ayuntamiento to sign 'subversive' papers. D

**C-A 19 (Dep. State Papers, Benicia (Military)), scan n251–n288** · Sav p. 246–283 · tomo p. 374–486 · 1831-08-05 · verbatim transcription · 

Causa criminal vs. José Mariano Duarte, Alcalde of San José, for getting the ayuntamiento to sign 'subversive' papers. Denounced by síndico Luis Chabolla[?]; the drafts (in the archive, extracted by Regidor Flores) deemed 'subversivos y de mucha malicia.' Doc nº1 (in the Alcalde's own hand): a federalist manifesto — 'este Ayuntam.to ha llegado á conocer sus derechos y el alcance de sus fuerzas' — calling (per constitutional art. 27) for the Exma Diputación to be convened at Monterey at the ayuntamientos' petition. Doc nº2: accusations the alcalde was caught in 'tratos ilícitos' with Ignacia Padilla at night (regidores Joaquín Higuera + Fernando Félix expelling him). ⚠️ The political agitation echoes the Solís Plan's central demand (reconvene the Diputación) and is an apparent precursor to the Nov-1831 anti-Victoria revolt. Docs nº3–4: the acta (1 June 1831) expanding the San José ayuntamiento — adding 2 regidores (Fernando Félix & Cnt. Rodríguez), secretary José Reyes Berreyesa, depositario the retired sgt Luis Peralta (the prominent ranchero). Witness Luis Chabolla (síndico): Duarte's circular invited the Territory's ayuntamientos to press the Cmdt. Gen. to convene the Diputación Provincial ('redunda en la felicidad del país'); Chabolla demurred (the matter belonged to the Diputación itself). Also alleged arbitrary acts (jailing a woman & asking Santa Clara's padre to shackle/shear her — the padre refused) + a municipal-funds dispute. Escribano Petronilo Ríos. Secretary José Reyes Berreyesa refused to copy/sign paper nº1, warning it meant attacking the Jefe Superior (Gov. Victoria); the Alcalde nonetheless sent the copy to Antonino Buelna (alcalde at Monterey — a 1831-revolt figure). Witness Mª Ignacia Padilla (true name Mª Presentación Lóngora[?]) detailed the alcalde's mistreatment (shackling her in jail). ⭐⭐⭐ Duarte's confession (n264–66): the plan originated at Monterey on 7 Apr 1831 in conversation with Antonino Buelna (Monterey alcalde), the Secretary of the Provincial Diputación [= J. B. Alvarado], and José Castro — to petition the Cmdt. Gen. to convene the Diputación; Buelna later came to San José, and Duarte signed the document Buelna forwarded to Monterey. (So the San José case ties directly into the Buelna / Castro / Alvarado federalist circle behind the Nov-1831 anti-Victoria revolt — the same young Californios as the Solís pasquines, Doc 96.) Fiscal Pliego: Duarte guilty by his own confession + the witnesses; the case moved to Monterey to depose the implicated Antonino Buelna (19 Aug), who denied remembering the Diputación plan. Defender Tte. Juan Mª Ibarra. José Castro (Monterey ayuntamiento secretary) & J. B. Alvarado (Diputación secretary) both deposed — distancing themselves from a direct alcalde-to-alcalde conspiracy while confirming the Monterey ayuntamiento DID form actas to convene the Diputación. ⭐⭐ Gov. Victoria's framing (24 Aug oficio): the agitation was 'procurar por medios ilegales se reuniese la Diputación, con fines siniestros y muy contrarios á las determinaciones del Sup.o Gob.,' with Buelna the key inducer — the government casting the federalist movement as subversion (the tension that exploded into the Nov-1831 revolt). The fiscal's summary (n280–85) restates the 3 charges: (1) pressing the ayuntamientos to convene the Diputación (a paper in Duarte's own hand, f.2); (2) his 7 Jul suspension over the night-time illicit-relations incident (f.3); (3) the arbitrary 1 June election of 2 regidores + secretary + depositario (f.5). ⚠️ n275–n285 gap-sampled (witness ratifications/careos + the summary; payload captured — completeness pass could fill these leaves). ⭐⭐ Conclusion (n286–88): the Asesor ruled the military lacked jurisdiction over alcaldes acting as first-instance judges, so the case was remitted to the Mexican Supreme Court (ministers Flores/Navarrete/Guzmán, Feb–Mar 1832) for civil handling + a Jefe-Político report on the alcaldes' 'crime' of trying to convene the Diputación. ⚠️ By then Victoria had already been overthrown — the prosecution was moot.
