# Doc II-64 — Hermenegildo Sal → acting Gov. Arrillaga — English ship (Capt. Brown) at SF, and the English at Bodega.

**C-A 54 (St. Pap. Sacramento, Tomo II), scan n261–n263** · orig. pp. 682–7 · Sav 262–264 · 1793, Mar 16, San Francisco · detail Transcription

Doc II-64 — Hermenegildo Sal → acting Gov. Arrillaga — English ship (Capt. Brown) at SF, and the English at Bodega.

> [!note] ⭐⭐⭐ Bodega-article document. Sal reports a large English vessel off the SF mouth, 13 Mar 1793; 6 men landed at Cantil Blanco — English, captain named "Brown," coming from San Luis, bound for Nootka, begging wood/water/meat without entering port. The ship lingered 10 days "sin perder de vista á Monterey y San Francisco," fired a shot under English colors, was seen among the Farallones. ⭐⭐ Sal's assessment: this ship + a *balandra* that put in at Monterey went to Bodega, and "por los naturales he sabido que aquí [Bodega] estuvieron más de seis meses" — indigenous intelligence that the English had frequented Bodega for 6+ months. Sal keeps livestock withdrawn, a Sunday lookout, "sin perder instante de hacer correrías." → [[bodega-failure-1793]] (English at Bodega, 1793; extends the i.119 boundary-letter intelligence), [[vancouver-voyage-discovery-1798]], [[sal-papers-project]], indigenous-intelligence (the "guarda isleño" reporting ship movements). *(Capt. Brown likely William Brown of the Butterworth NW-coast traders — verify.)*
