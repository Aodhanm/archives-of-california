# Doc IV-42 — Hermenegildo Sal → Gov. Arrillaga — his apology for Vancouver's Santa Clara trip.

**C-A 54 (St. Pap. Sacramento, Tomo IV), scan n406–n407** · orig. pp. 27–31 · Sav 407–408 · 1793, Apr 30, San Francisco · detail Transcription

Doc IV-42 — Hermenegildo Sal → Gov. Arrillaga — his apology for Vancouver's Santa Clara trip.

> [!note] ⭐ After Arrillaga's reprimand (oficio of 26 Mar), Sal apologizes for the "mal procedimiento" of having permitted "el capitán Inglés [Vancouver] con otros oficiales se hubieren internado hasta Santa Clara" — "caí en un yerro." Pleads that Vancouver's "persuasiones" don't excuse him; Padre Fray Martín de Landaeta also observed Vancouver's "mal modo"; the only way to have stopped it would have been to take away the presidio's horses. ⭐ The disciplinary aftermath of the Vancouver visit — Spanish command anxiety over Sal's accommodation of the British (cf. Doc 53). → [[bodega-failure-1793]], [[vancouver-voyage-discovery-1798]], [[sal-papers-project]].
