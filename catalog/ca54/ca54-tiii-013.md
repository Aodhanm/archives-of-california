# Doc III-13 — Hermenegildo Sal → acting Gov. Arrillaga — "Sobre la expedición de Vancouver."

**C-A 54 (St. Pap. Sacramento, Tomo III), scan n303–n305** · orig. pp. 264–319 · Sav 304–306 · 1792, Nov 30, San Francisco · detail Transcription

Doc III-13 — Hermenegildo Sal → acting Gov. Arrillaga — "Sobre la expedición de Vancouver."

> [!note] ⭐⭐⭐ The fullest Sal narrative of Vancouver's SF visit (Nov 1792). The frigate Discovery (Capt. George Vancouver) anchored at Yerba Buena on the 13th in 4½ brazas; Sal sent Alférez Alejo Miranda as pilot "con orden de no recibir ninguna gratificación"; on the 19th Vancouver rode to Santa Clara with an escort, 7 officers, Sgt. Pedro Amador & 8 soldiers on horses Sal furnished; the brig "Chatham" under "Puget" ("supongo es Broughton") arrived the 18th. ⭐ Provisioning list (some on the San Blas commander's account, much "dado gratis por él" [Sal]): 11 + 2 cows, 2 calves, mutton, 10 arrobas lard, 30 pumpkins, 40 sacks of vegetables, 70 hens + 25 capons. ⭐ Separate note: the brig "Chatham" (21st) — "Don Juan de la Cuadra [Bodega y Quadra], no habiéndose remitido lo que le prometió" (the promised guns), Sal asks for a fortification. → [[bodega-failure-1793]], [[vancouver-voyage-discovery-1798]], [[sal-papers-project]]. *(Plus a Sal note on Mission Santa Cruz being moved 300 paces uphill after flooding — Savage dates it "1802" but content [="el año anterior"] + Sal's authorship ⚠️ → almost certainly 1792.)*
