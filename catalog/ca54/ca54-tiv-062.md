# the Colorado/Yuma Indians' overtures to the missions.

**C-A 54 (St. Pap. Sacramento, Tomo IV), scan n463–n468** · orig. pp. 309–11 · Sav 464–468 · 1800, Jul, San Miguel · detail Transcription

the Colorado/Yuma Indians' overtures to the missions.

> [!note] ⭐ Ignacio Pacheco → Sal (2 Jul 1800): ~15 Colorado-River gentiles reached a ranchería near the Tulares, bearing blankets & buckskin shirts to lure neophytes to the missions; one questioned the padre "por qué pegaba y hacía trabajar tanto á los cristianos" and urged returning the Christians to their lands. ⭐⭐ Gabriel Moraga's investigation (11 Jul 1800) — conflicting native testimony: one informant (Pomarca) says the Yumas threatened war on Missions San Miguel & San Luis; the *capitanejo* denies any threat — only the annual trade (blankets/shirts for beads), 11 Indians. → [[indian-raiding-article]], [[indigenous-intelligence-project]] (indigenous diplomacy + the Spanish reading conflicting native intelligence).
