# military discipline.

**C-A 54 (St. Pap. Sacramento, Tomo II), scan n174–n175** · orig. pp. 203–8 · Sav 175–176 · 1785, Apr 15 / Jul 1, Monterey · detail Transcription

military discipline.

> [!note] (1) Every party leaving the presidio/port to march well-escorted. (2) ⭐⭐ Cmdt.-Gen.'s order (via Fages): the officers & troops behave with "una libertad vecina muy perjudicial por los desórdenes escandalosos en que incurren con las indias cristianas y gentiles" — circulate a *repressive* order with grave penalties, "nada disimule en este punto." (3) The settlement-of-sailors policy from the Reglamento (Art. 2, tít. 3 — fill vacancies from ship crews; admit voluntary settlers). ⭐ Spanish command's own admission of soldiers' sexual abuse of Indian women — significant for indigenous/social history. → [[indian-raiding-article]], social history.
