# the Otter incident (first U.S. ship in CA).

**C-A 54 (St. Pap. Sacramento, Tomo IV), scan n387–n388** · orig. pp. 696–713 · Sav 388–389 · 1796, Nov 10, Monterey · detail Transcription

the Otter incident (first U.S. ship in CA).

> [!note] ⭐⭐⭐ The American frigate (Savage "el otro Boston"; Captain "Door" = Ebenezer Dorr of the *Otter*) — the first U.S. ship to visit California, Oct–Nov 1796 — after being refused permission, forcibly landed English convicts/deserters at gunpoint on deserted beaches "fuera de la vista de la batería" (5 on 6 Nov, 5 more on 7 Nov, "con un par de pistolas en la mano"). ⭐ Borica's admission: with no naval force and only "30 ó 40 hombres," he "no podía resguardar la parte de costa que hay de este puerto para abajo." The stranded English (carpenters, smiths, tailors, sailors) set to work at 1½ reales/day. → [[bay-area-borderlands project]] (American perspective; the open coast), [[presidio-decay-project]]. ⚠️ Verify "Dorr"/"Otter" reading.
