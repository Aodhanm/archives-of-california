# Royal Order on foreign whaling-contraband ships.

**C-A 54 (St. Pap. Sacramento, Tomo II), scan n190–n191** · orig. pp. 304 · Sav 191–192 · 1803, Aug 10, Madrid · detail Transcription

Royal Order on foreign whaling-contraband ships.

> [!note] The companion metropolitan order: foreign ships "so color de la pesca de la Ballena hacen un excedido contrabando" on the Mar del Sur coasts of the King's dominions; coastal employees to bar foreigners from landing per standing orders, "siendo este punto convenido entre todas las potencias que tienen colonias"; ships caught smuggling — the named "Ulises" and "Stefan" — to be confiscated. ⭐⭐ The King is notifying the Spanish Minister to the United States of America to warn that Government that action will be taken against [ships] landing at the Spanish colonies. ⭐ Strong borderlands evidence — Spain at the metropolitan level (1803) addressing the U.S. directly over American/foreign whalers & contraband on the Pacific/California coast. → [[bay-area-borderlands project]] (American perspective / foreign incursion), [[otter-corruption-project]].
