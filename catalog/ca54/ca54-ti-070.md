# transmits a copy of Felipe de Neve's *Instrucción* to his successor Pedro Fages.

**C-A 54 (St. Pap. Sacramento, Tomo I), scan n72–~n105** · orig. pp. 72–105 · Sav 73–106 · 1792, May 10, San Diego *(text dates to Neve's 1782 handover)* · detail Transcription (long; gap-sampled n73, 76, 79–80, 84, 88, 94, 100, 102)

transmits a copy of Felipe de Neve's *Instrucción* to his successor Pedro Fages.

> [!note] Nava forwards the full text of ex-Gov. Felipe de Neve's handover *Instrucción* to Gov. Pedro Fages (Neve governed 1777–82), because the original was missing from the Cal. government archive (per the gov.'s oficio No. 88 of 21 Jan 1792). A long, article-numbered governance document (arts. 1–18+) spanning ~34 Savage pages. ⭐⭐ Major source for early-1780s administration of *both* Californias. Sampled content: (core policy) "El principal objeto del acierto de Gobierno debe consistir en conservar en una perfecta paz y tranquilidad la Península, manteniendo la amistad y buen trato de todas las Naciones gentiles sin permitir se les persiga en modo alguno" — conciliation via small gifts ("uno á dos hilos de abalorios"). (Colorado/Yuma frontier, n76) the Quechan attacks — killing of Cabo Antonio Briones, fires set before Alférez José Velásquez's 50-mule pack-train; Neve's planned 40-man *entrada* "para sorprenderlos en sus rancherías… sin hacerles guerra" was undone by "la novedad ocurrida en el Colorado" (the 1781 Yuma Revolt). (graduated Indian discipline, n79–81) for stock-killing near Monterey/SF/San José/San Diego: 8–10 days *cepo* + 20–25 lashes for gentiles, *then* admonish & gift an *almud* of maize; names Fr. Junípero Serra (asked to admonish San Carlos neophytes who killed ~10 head of the Monterey *caballada*) and Fr. Peña at Santa Clara; rule that no religious accompany sorties to gentile rancherías. (presidio economy, n88) *matanza*/tallow/*tasajo* provisioning to wean the presidios off San Blas *manteca*. (Baja fiscal, n94–101) Loreto *alcabala*; the Real de Santa Ana silver *hacienda* sold to Capt. Antonio de Osio ($1,000); José de Gálvez's *bando* barring commerce with the Manila *nao*; mission-inventory accounting. (art. 17) a proposed Peninsula–Philippines commerce plan; (art. 18) relations with the Padre Presidente. → [[bay-area-borderlands project]], [[indian-raiding-article]], governance. ⚠️ Long doc — only sampled; a future pass could transcribe arts. fully.
