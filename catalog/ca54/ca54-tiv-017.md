# the Colorado campaign vs. the Yumas.

**C-A 54 (St. Pap. Sacramento, Tomo IV), scan n345–n352** · orig. pp. 21–27 · Sav 346–353 · 1781, Nov 15, Arispe · detail Transcription

the Colorado campaign vs. the Yumas.

> [!note] ⭐ The military response to the 1781 Yuma Revolt: the war council (Cmdt.-Gen., Intendant Pedro Corbalán, auditor Pedro Galindo) reads Neve's & Fages's Colorado-River dispatches; Gov. Fages to take command of the punitive expedition, "castigar y sujetar á los pérfidos y cobardes Yumas," conduct campaign or "paces honrosas," treat the allied "Naciones Amigas" well, letting them keep the booty (horses, mules, cattle), reconnoiter the Colorado for a new presidio + settlement. ⭐ The "3.ª Resolución" requires the death or surrender of the capitanejo Salvador Palma (the Quechan leader) + the interpreter Francisco Xavier before any peace. → [[indian-raiding-article]], military history, borderlands (Colorado frontier).
