# ⭐⭐ Long *sumaria* (Sgt. Fco. de Aguiar) on the disorders in the San Juan Capistrano & San Gabriel escorts — soldiers goi

**C-A 55 (St. Pap. Sacramento VI–IX), scan n257–n264+** · tomo p. viii.231–250+ · Sav p. 258–265+ · 1777-07-11 (+ 26 May *sumaria*) · verbatim transcription · ⭐⭐⭐⭐

⭐⭐ Long *sumaria* (Sgt. Fco. de Aguiar) on the disorders in the San Juan Capistrano & San Gabriel escorts — soldiers going at night to the rancherías to solicit/force gentile women ("forzar á las mujeres de los gentiles"); Fran.º Villagómez twice violated "el 6º mandamiento" with the gentile captain's wife (she came to wash clothes at the *playa*; question whether by force or will) — put in irons; Mathías de la Vega, José/Fran.º Peña, Julián Acevedo, "Gloria," Soberanes named; ⭐ key motive: these soldiers "les han quitado las pieles de venado á los gentiles" (stripped the gentiles of their deer-skins), "cuyas maldades pueden producir muy malas consecuencias de perturbar la paz." Formal witness declarations (soldiers Acevedo, "Gloria," Juan Álvarez et al. — going to the rancherías, an *india* hurt) closing at San Juan Capistrano (21 Jun 1777, Guillermo Carrillo; cert. copy Ortega, San Diego Jul 1777) & the San Gabriel escort (1 Oct 1777). ⭐ Embeds a punitive skirmish at the ranchería *Alacua Chomi* (20 Jun 1777): a cabo + 5 soldiers attacked; gentiles loosed arrows (wounding Mathías López's & Acevedo's horses); soldiers fired, 3–4 Indians killed, 2 captured (one given 25 lashes); the gentiles had vowed to behead the "cabo guapo" & threatened mission Christians — over soldiers stripping them of deer-skins & forcing their women. The documented cause behind the SJC indigenous resistance (Docs 204–205, 231). Long *sumaria*, n257–n278, abstract pp.360–396
