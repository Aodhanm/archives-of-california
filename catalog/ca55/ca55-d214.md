# Bundle transcribing Viceroy Branciforte's orders. (Nº1–2) R. Cédula of 1 Apr: arms carried to Cádiz not in the ship's re

**C-A 55 (St. Pap. Sacramento VI–IX), scan n174–n188** · tomo p. vii.72–126 · Sav p. 175–189 · 1796-11-19 (→1797) · Savage abstract · ⭐

Bundle transcribing Viceroy Branciforte's orders. (Nº1–2) R. Cédula of 1 Apr: arms carried to Cádiz not in the ship's register liable to *comiso* + customs-certificate rule; (Nº3) collect chupas, cannon & 50 @ powder from the San Blas ships for Monterey; (Nº4) missions must furnish horses for extraordinary troop service; (Nº5–9) Monterey habilitación accounting (Alf. José Pérez Fernández); (Nº6) supply missions cloth & farm tools at cost; (Nº10) $2,122 from the *Cuenta Mayor de muertos* for the Monterey fort works (Habd. Manuel de Carcaba); (Nº11) bando re the French Nation (1796 war); (Nº12–13) the Compañía de Voluntarios de Cataluña + a 15-man artillery *piquete* to California (armed by the Veracruz treasury; pay via the California Síndico from 1796); (Nº14–15) advances to artisans Manuel Esteban Ruiz (mason/stonemason) & tailor Joaquín Botello ($1,000); ⭐ (Nº16) Borica's FOUR-ARTISAN PROGRAM to teach trades to the *naturales*: tailor Joaquín Botello, potter Mariano Tapia, ribbon-maker Manuel Muñoz, weaver Mariano Mendoza ($100 each; certified by Alberni); (Nº17) $300 ($75 ea.) for their *regalos*; (Nº18) carpenter Leocadio Martínez wounded the Monterey smith Pedro Gonzáles — merited 10 yrs presidio but took church asylum, sent to San José; (Nº19–21) accounting/liquidation & glosa orders for the Tribunal de Cuentas
