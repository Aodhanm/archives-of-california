# ⭐⭐ The Hartnell–McCulloch tallow contract: the *vecinos* beg to defer this year's *unto* tax (failed harvests); he grant

**C-A 55 (St. Pap. Sacramento VI–IX), scan n49** · tomo p. i.160–161 · Sav p. 50 · 1822-07-06 · Savage abstract · ⭐⭐

⭐⭐ The Hartnell–McCulloch tallow contract: the *vecinos* beg to defer this year's *unto* tax (failed harvests); he grants one year only, calling them vice-ridden; reports "los comerciantes ingleses Arnell y Macala" (Hartnell & McCulloch) contracted with the Prefecto de Misiones to take tallow (*sebo*) for 3 years at $1, soap at $2/arroba, *manteca* at $4, etc.; he got them to offer the same prices to the *vecinos* — a real/arroba of sales to benefit the pueblos & fund troop pay
