# VANCOUVER'S LONG LETTER — the Open Door articulated by the English commander: his voyage of discovery on the NW Coast of

**C-A 6 (Provincial State Papers), scan n285–n289** · Sav p. XI:101–105 · tomo p. (1793) · 1793-12-02 · Savage abstract · 

VANCOUVER'S LONG LETTER — the Open Door articulated by the English commander: his voyage of discovery on the NW Coast of America, its geography now 'asegurado hasta la latitud de los 56 grados al Norte y Longitud 226 grados al Oeste de Greenwich,' extending further N/W (must stay to finish 'el verano que viene'); the British Court told him before leaving England he'd be received with hospitality and that his reports to London would go 'por el conducto de los oficiales de S.M. Católica… por el camino de San Blas'; on the 'convites amigablemente hechos en Nootka' he came 'con la mayor confianza,' his expectations 'han excedido'; 'yo me considero más como criado del mundo que de un soberano particular'; he is instructed to freely share all discoveries with any Spanish official and to send Quadra copies of all his charts since leaving last year 'de esa manera la Corte de España ha de recibir la información de mis labores antes que la de la Gran Bretaña'; thanks for the Viceroy's letters & the favors to his crew; expects orders at San Diego about 'la entrega de Nutka [the Nootka restitution]'; frames Arrillaga's ports as 'uno de los Puertos… considerando como los de un amigo y aliado', the only harbors near his object for refreshments/crew-health vs. the cold north & the 'turbulent' Sandwich Islanders (cont.)
