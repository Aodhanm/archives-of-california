# 'Comercio en China, Pesca de Nutrias' — a formal 3-part inspector's report. (1) the *utilidades* to troops/settlers if t

**C-A 4 (Provincial State Papers), scan n138–n139** · Sav p. 139–140 · tomo p. 136+ · 1787-01-14 · verbatim transcription · 

'Comercio en China, Pesca de Nutrias' — a formal 3-part inspector's report. (1) the *utilidades* to troops/settlers if the prohibition on trading the Manila-galleon's China goods at Monterey were lifted — judges them 'mínguas ó muy cortas' (troops have almost no cash beyond their *mantas cambayas*); (2) who'd benefit (friars/soldiers/settlers, ~300 p.); (3) proposes keeping the trade prohibited except when necessity requires, and that 'si la peletería [otter-pelt trade] fuere libre sería otra cosa' — a candid administration-side read of the China trade + otter fishery at the capital. Orig. p.136+ (Savage p.139).
