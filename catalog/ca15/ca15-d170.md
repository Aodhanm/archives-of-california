# ⭐⭐ Memorandum on the presidial horse-herd situados (caballadas) & frontier cavalry doctrine. Argues the heavy company si

**C-A 15 (Prov. St. Pap. Benicia Mil.), scan n138–n144** · orig. p. 9–44 · Sav p. 136–142 · 1780-04-03 · unspecified · ⭐⭐

⭐⭐ Memorandum on the presidial horse-herd situados (caballadas) & frontier cavalry doctrine. Argues the heavy company situado (346 horses, 67 mules) is unsustainable & self-defeating: constant field service ruins the soldier's kit & indebts him, leaves no time for drill, the penal law, religion, or to see wife & children — "un hombre hambriento que lleva una vida miserable y abandonada." The Apaches "buscan siempre apoderarse de caballos y mulas"; once robbed, the troops are left "á pie" and "cesan sus operaciones por muchos meses." Remedy: reduce each soldier to 2 horses + 1 mule kept stable-fed (caballerizas). Includes a frontier-wide allotment table: 18 regular presidios 2,284 horses/1,152 mules; 4 Nueva Vizcaya flying cos. 1,184/592; Sonora 92/46; dragoon piquetes; Sta Fe (NM) 224/112; San Antonio de Béxar 188/94; Bahía del Espíritu Santo 120/60; 30 Rosales auxiliaries 60/30 — total 4,332 horses, 2,266 mules = 6,598. Company effective strength: 71 plazas → 61 effective (guardia de caballada 20, escolta de prisiones/caudales 15, correos/enfermos/presos 8 → 21 left). Copia legalizada by Cap. Bonilla. Orig. pp.9–44 (vide pp.12–14).
