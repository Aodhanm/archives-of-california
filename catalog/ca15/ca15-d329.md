# ⭐⭐ Opinion on the New California poblador livestock-grant policy & the tithe/cattle-disposal dispute. Each settler was g

**C-A 15 (Prov. St. Pap. Benicia Mil.), scan n238–n240** · orig. p. 235–240 · Sav p. 233–235 · 1788-02-12 · unspecified · ⭐⭐

⭐⭐ Opinion on the New California poblador livestock-grant policy & the tithe/cattle-disposal dispute. Each settler was given (as ventajas or "por poblador") 2 mares, 2 cows with a calf, 2 ewes & 2 goats, to graze together & breed for 2 years (no killing or selling), then to build to 15 mares + a stallion, 15 cows + a bull, 12 ewes + a ram, 10 nanny-goats + a billy. Debates whether settlers retain the old liberty to dispose of their stock (the tithe-holders would limit it); concludes only male animals may be culled as needed, the Government guarding against abuses. A key document on the founding ranching economy & livestock distribution to settlers. Orig. pp.235–240.
