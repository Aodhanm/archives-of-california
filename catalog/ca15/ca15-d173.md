# ⭐⭐⭐ On the disadvantages of the large caballada situado & the rising military parity of the frontier Indians. Forwards h

**C-A 15 (Prov. St. Pap. Benicia Mil.), scan n147–n149** · orig. p. 57–65 · Sav p. 145–147 · 1780-09-22 · unspecified · ⭐⭐⭐

⭐⭐⭐ On the disadvantages of the large caballada situado & the rising military parity of the frontier Indians. Forwards his memo on the drawbacks of horse-herd situados (vide pp.3–9) for opinion; argues a California soldier needs fewer than 7 horses + 1 mule given his lighter fatigues vs. Sonora. Striking verbatim passage: when the enemy Indians were first encountered they "ignoraban el uso de nuestros caballos y armas" and 2 soldiers could rout hundreds — but now "todos los indios bárbaros que nos hacen la guerra saben ya el uso del arma de fuego, y algunas naciones la poseen con más acierto y seguridad que nuestras tropas mal disciplinadas, habiendo adelantado en el de sus flechas, lanzas y macanas"; they attack in equal numbers, dispute actions, rove the royal roads at will — "se acabó el tiempo en que un pequeño número de nuestros soldados se hacía respetar de multitud de indios." After 40 years of war the frontier costs the King >$804,000/year. Closes: ceasing horse/mule breeding in California & the frontier provinces would deter foreigners from penetrating these dominions (they would lack transport across the intervening deserts). Orig. pp.57–65.
