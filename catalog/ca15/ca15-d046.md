# ⭐⭐⭐ The Pamó conspiracy & firing-squad execution of 4 Indian captains. On 16 Mar, Benito, an Indian capitán, warned (via

**C-A 15 (Prov. St. Pap. Benicia Mil.), scan n42–n45** · orig. p. 737–764, 767–769 · Sav p. 43–46 · 1778-04-06 · verbatim transcription · ⭐⭐⭐

⭐⭐⭐ The Pamó conspiracy & firing-squad execution of 4 Indian captains. On 16 Mar, Benito, an Indian capitán, warned (via Fr. Lasuén) that the Indians of Pamó — esp. their capitán Aarán[?] — were gathering arms to kill Christians; 3 neighbouring captains + others "del otro lado de la sierra" were in the plot, which had killed a fugitive from San Juan Capistrano. Aarán defied Ortega, daring the soldiers to come "para cogerlos á mano y matarlos á macanazos." Sargto Mariano Carrillo (+ cabo, 8 soldiers, 2 guides) surprised Pamó at 3am on 31 Mar; the captains were seized & bound; 2 Indians killed, a few burned in a house refusing to surrender; the rest submitted & were flogged. >1,500 arrows, 80+ bows & many war-clubs taken & burned. The 4 captains — Achil, Aalcuirin[?], Aarán, Taguaqui[?] — examined 4 Apr, convicted of conspiring to kill the Christians despite a royal pardon, "por haber juntado gente… para pelear con los de la Soledad después de la destrucción de la Misión" [1775 SD revolt]. ⚠️ Ortega's sentence (verbatim): "los condeno á que el día 11 á las 9 de la mañana mueran con la violencia de dos tiros de escopeta, presente la tropa armada en guerra, y todas las rancherías cristianas… para que les sirva de estímulo á obrar bien." And to the friars (re baptism): "si se reducen á las aguas del Santo Bautismo, mueran el Sábado 11… por la mañana, y si no, también." Lasuén began instructing them; the condemned disposed to be baptized. (Savage notes a discrepancy in the certified copy.) Orig. pp.737–764, 767–769.
