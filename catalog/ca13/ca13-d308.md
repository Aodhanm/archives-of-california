# ⭐⭐ Gov. Solá → the presidio commandants — the foundational Bouchard-defense circular + full mobilization/evacuation *ord

**C-A 13 (Prov. St. Pap. XX, 1816–22), scan n245–n252** · tomo p. i.1090–1093 · Sav p. 246–253 · 1818-10-08 · Savage abstract · ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

⭐⭐ Gov. Solá → the presidio commandants — the foundational Bouchard-defense circular + full mobilization/evacuation *ordenanza* (signed Monterey 8 Oct 1818). Warning (via Guerra, from an American ship): 2 pirate frigates (34 guns / 18–20 guns, 350 men) left the Sandwich Islands, bound for this coast in 4–6 days. Plan: secure all church silver/gold to named inland depots (SF→San José; Monterey→Soledad; San Luis→Sta Margarita; Purísima→San Antonio rancho; SD→San Antonio de Pala…); cache provisions; evacuate women & children; drive cattle & horse-herds inland; muster discharged men & invalids (Luis Argüello at SF; Ortega & Mariano Castro ranchos); a flying squad w/ 300-cartridge reserve; disperse 2/3 of the powder inland; ⭐ spike the cannons if abandoned; double mounted-Indian lookouts; ⭐⭐ a statewide mounted-courier relay route (SF→San Mateo→San José→Castro's rancho→Monterey→Soledad→San Antonio→San Miguel→San Luis→Purísima→Sta Inés→Sta Bárbara→San Buenaventura…, relay-padres Oliva/Cabot/Ripoll/Sánchez); free the prisoners for service; Guerra named 2nd-in-command of arms / accidental governor if Solá falls. = expanded Document 21
