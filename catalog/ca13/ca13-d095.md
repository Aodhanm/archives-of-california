# José de la Guerra y Noriega → Gov. Solá (long): defends Santiago Argüello against the San Juan Capistrano fathers' compl

**C-A 13 (Prov. St. Pap. XX, 1816–22), scan n87–n90** · tomo p. i.331–334 (& 332, 333/334 dup.) · Sav p. 88–91 · 1819-04-06 · Savage abstract · 

José de la Guerra y Noriega → Gov. Solá (long): defends Santiago Argüello against the San Juan Capistrano fathers' complaint that, during the Bouchard invasion alarm, the mission escort/guard was withdrawn to a defensive point and the mission houses locked, leaving it unguarded; Guerra argues the circumstances (invasion threat, need to concentrate force) justified Argüello, faults the fathers' grievance; Anastasio Carrillo also implicated; encloses the replies
