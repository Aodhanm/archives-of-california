**C-A 22 (Provincial Records Vol. I), scan n130–n132** · tomo p. 273–276 · Sav p. 130–132 · 1779-06-04 · Savage abstract · ⭐

⭐ *Misiones de la Antigua California*: the plan to settle the Baja Indians in pueblos (lots, lands, cattle-roundups so they maintain themselves) — entrusted to Rivera y Moncada & Pres. Vicente de Mora, who "con errada inteligencia" moved families to San José del Cabo & Todos Santos; ethnography of Santiago (only 70 souls), SJ del Cabo, Todos Santos, Comondú, Mulegé — fed from royal cattle, working fields only a few days then dispersing to the hills (vandijas, roots, wild fruit), at the mission only on feast days; "de esta manera no pueden civilizarse y ser útiles al Estado"
