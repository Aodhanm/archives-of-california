# BOUNDED BLOCK — routine governor's orders (seeds to feed Indians; preceptor appointments Bonilla/Amado $1,000/$15; the $

**C-A 51 (State Papers, Missions), scan n185–192** · Sav p. IX·8–15 · tomo p. — · 1834–1841 · Savage abstract · 

BOUNDED BLOCK — routine governor's orders (seeds to feed Indians; preceptor appointments Bonilla/Amado $1,000/$15; the $500-worship-allowance circular; Jimeno Casarín commissioned to secularize SLO) + Fr. Ramón Abella's *amancebamiento* accusation vs Martín Cooper & the preceptor Amado (San Miguel 1835); CENTERPIECE = ⭐⭐⭐ the 'Nota: Vicente Gómez dice…' (n187–191) — a damning analytical indictment of secularization administration: the missions fell into misery because the governors appointed 'hombres ineptos… de mala fe, solo por favoritismo' who enriched their own families at the missions' expense, were given to 'embriaguez y el juego,' distributed livestock to cronies, bled the establishments via the govt's repeated asset-transfer orders, and covered up cattle-rustling/hide-theft. ⚠ NOT a primary 1830s doc — it is Vicente Gómez's RETROSPECTIVE editorial note (the Bancroft-era Californio scribe/informant); cite as his interpretation, not contemporary evidence → [[indian-raiding-article]] / [[californio-article]] secularization historiography
