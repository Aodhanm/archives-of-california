# BOUNDED — the friars' secularization-era correspondence: (1) ⭐ Fr. Antonio Flores y Real handing Santa Cruz to del Valle

**C-A 51 (State Papers, Missions), scan n264–268** · Sav p. X·6–10 · tomo p. orig pp.223–359 · 1822–1842 · Savage abstract · 

BOUNDED — the friars' secularization-era correspondence: (1) ⭐ Fr. Antonio Flores y Real handing Santa Cruz to del Valle (1834) — 6 requests incl. that Indians be compelled to doctrina/mass because on the feast of Santa Cruz 'dejaron al padre solo en el altar con 3 indios y 4 ó 5 mujeres' (neophyte religious abandonment at secularization); (2) ⭐⭐ Fr. Rafael de Jesús Moreno (Santa Clara, 1836→Castro): newcomers from the republic 'se figuran que en California se les ha de dar todo de balde, y que los indios nacen para ser esclavos de ellos' (quotable settler-exploitation critique; also aids an officer bound for *la Bodega* → russian-california); (3) Fr. Moreno's protest over the withheld $500 worship allowance (Zacatecan college; 'contra los sagrados cánones… y la facultad 12 de la Constitución'); (4) Frs. Narciso Durán & Antonio Jimeno (Sta Bárbara) deny 'completamente falsas' the accusations that friars meddle in govt to set admin vs neophytes; (5) padrones — SLO end-1834 = 253 souls (⚠ 59 widowers vs 7 widows) [Abella/Arroyo de la Cuesta]; San Buenaventura handover; (6) ⭐ 1822 independence oaths to the Mexican Empire by Frs. Felipe Arroyo de la Cuesta & Esteban Tapis (SJB)
