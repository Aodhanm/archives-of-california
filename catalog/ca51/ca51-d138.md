# BOUNDED BLOCK — comisionado Ignacio del Valle secularizes ex-Mission Santa Cruz into the 'Pueblo de Figueroa': forms a p

**C-A 51 (State Papers, Missions), scan n245–251** · Sav p. IX·68–74 · tomo p. orig pp.634–739 · 1834–1841 · Savage abstract · 

BOUNDED BLOCK — comisionado Ignacio del Valle secularizes ex-Mission Santa Cruz into the 'Pueblo de Figueroa': forms a parish separate from San Juan Bautista ($1,000 congrua each minister); a room-dispute with the Padre Ministro (friar wanted 10 rooms + the granary as a stable, Figueroa limits him, keeps the *troje* for community seed); ⭐ del Valle gives ALL the Santa Cruz Indians surnames ('apelativos') one Sunday and renames the pueblo after Figueroa (n248) → neophyte-identity/secularization; balance sheet in the red ($1,877 assets vs $4,979 liabilities); Juan González 1838–39 account (deficit $345; cattle handed to S. Juan Bautista + 3 mules to the foreigner Green). Then La Soledad opens (n250): admin Salvador Espinosa padrón 1837 = 177 souls; tiny monthly accounts (existencia ~$54–90); Vicente Cantúa 1841 inventory (570 wild cattle, small herd)
