# BOUNDED — two strong resistance finds: (1) ⭐⭐⭐ Temecula/Pala (San Luis Rey), Nov 1840 — via the Juzgado (Ríos/Ruiz): the

**C-A 51 (State Papers, Missions), scan n261–263** · Sav p. X·3–5 · tomo p. orig pp.117–209 · 1834–1840 · Savage abstract · 

BOUNDED — two strong resistance finds: (1) ⭐⭐⭐ Temecula/Pala (San Luis Rey), Nov 1840 — via the Juzgado (Ríos/Ruiz): the Pala alcalde Nepomuceno & 'Capitán Juan', having consulted their people, respond unánimes that they will not admit Pío Pico (nor his brother Andrés) nor their cattle onto Temecula; 'toda la gente está armada,' intent only 'á impedir que los Picos tomen posesión de dicho rancho' (not to oppose the authorities); 'los indios no quieren ceder sus derechos' over Temecula 'el que da más semillas para la misión'; await the govt's ruling, armed but 'tranquila.' Pairs with Santiago Argüello relaying the Gov's June-1840 provisional grant of Temecula to the Picos (n262) → [[indian-raiding-article]] Indian land-rights + [[californio-article]] Pico secularization land-grab. ⚠ transcribe n261 verbatim. (2) ⭐⭐ José de Jesús Vallejo (San José, Aug 1838) captures & has shot ('fusilar') Ambrosio, chief of the Muquelemne (Mokelumne) ranchería recognized as *jefe*, after Ambrosio's armed band threatened killings at Rancho las Borregas; Fr. J.J. González gave him last rites → [[military-history-project]] + indian-raiding. + SLR 1834 matanza (5,700 reses, 2,850 hides to mission / half to porcioneros)
