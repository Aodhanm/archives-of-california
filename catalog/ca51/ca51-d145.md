# BOUNDED — (1) ⭐⭐ José Castro → Figueroa (San Juan Bautista, 1835), anticlerical secularization broadside: regrets the be

**C-A 51 (State Papers, Missions), scan n273–277** · Sav p. X·15–20 · tomo p. orig pp.492–728 · 1835–1841 · Savage abstract · 

BOUNDED — (1) ⭐⭐ José Castro → Figueroa (San Juan Bautista, 1835), anticlerical secularization broadside: regrets the best building was ceded to Fr. Antonio Anzar (already holding 14 rooms) — 'el interés que estos Caballeros tienen en entorpecer todo adelanto… la ambición y malicia dominan al corazón de estos hombres' → [[californio-article]] secularization politics (the Californio-secularizer counterpoint to the friar complaints of Docs 142); (2) ⭐⭐ Padrón de los indígenas EMANCIPADOS at San Juan Bautista (Antonio Buelna / José Tiburcio Castro, 9 May 1835) — 63 individuals w/ itemized value of land ($5,120) + cattle/oxen/horses/tools/clothing/hides allotted to each → concrete secularization property-distribution / Indian land ownership → [[californio-article]]; (3) ⭐⭐ San Miguel mayordomo poisoned & killed by the neophyte 'Camuto' (a Christian Tulareño of the Pontach ranchería), caught w/ an accomplice (Inocente García, Aug 1839) → [[indian-raiding-article]] violent resistance; + Dolores padrón 1841 = 78 souls (nearly depopulated; livestock 1,514 head; Tiburcio Vásquez) + admin resignations (Estrada/San José, Bonilla/San Miguel). ⭐ Savage's '(fin)' at n277 bounds Tomo X's remainder as routine receipts/loose accounts
