**C-A 34 (Departmental State Papers, Los Angeles), scan n539–n540** · Sav p.  · tomo p. 539–540 · 1840-06-09 · Savage abstract · 

transcribes Carrillo's campaign parte — the 36-man party set out 3 a.m. 23 May, tracked the raiders (who had a 4-day start with ~3,000 head) north; on 24 May reached 'ojo de agua del rincón,' found a fresh trail; the raiders (30+, all rifleros), warned by scouts, fled precipitately from 'agua de Rincón,' abandoning gear, rope & ~500 head dead of hunger & thirst; not pursued further for the state of the horses & want of food (= the session-1 gap-sample E2) (doc pp.492-498)
