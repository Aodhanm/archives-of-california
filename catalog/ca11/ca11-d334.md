# ★ ARRILLAGA MISSION REPORT (cont. from deep read pp. 337–340) — ~17 pages total. FOOD: proportional to abundance; some n

**C-A 11 (Prov. St. Pap. XVIII), scan n341–354** · 1804 Nov 10 · unspecified

★ ARRILLAGA MISSION REPORT (cont. from deep read pp. 337–340) — ~17 pages total. FOOD: proportional to abundance; some neophytes "dispatched" to forage. CLOTHING: "el gentil siempre anda desnudo"; cotones/blankets sufficient; looms since *Concepción*. HOUSING: prefers chozas for health; infirmería/Casa de Solteras reformed. LABOR: work "sumamente flojo"; women more docile; denies forced labor of pregnant women. PUNISHMENT: stocks, shackles, dark rooms, calabozos exist; "the common punishment is azotes y grillos"; "in my estimation the Padres lack more indulgence than rigor." MISSIONARY SALES: few excesses; defends missionaries' right to wine/aguardiente — "they do well to have it in such unhappy destinations... scolding a rational species of second class." MISSION FINANCES: libranzas for 18 Alta Cal. missions total 12–18,000 pesos/year. DEFENSE OF SYSTEM: punishment "has its origin in the very conquest of Antigua California, practiced for more than 100 years"; "varying it could have fatal consequences." PRESIDENTIAL AUTHORITY: witnessed 5 consecutive President appointments in Baja Cal.; missionaries shown cuaderno of 10–16 pages of spiritual faculties. Signed: "I consider I have been somewhat diffuse and tiresome." pp. 835–41
