# ⭐⭐⭐ The longest expediente in the volume (~LXVI pp. 90–390, dragging from Aug 1828 into 1831). Causa criminal for the mu

**C-A 18 (Dep. St. Pap. Benicia Mil., 1822–28), scan n327–n386+** · tomo p. LXVI.~90–390 · 1828-08-16 → 1831 · Savage abstract · ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

⭐⭐⭐ The longest expediente in the volume (~LXVI pp. 90–390, dragging from Aug 1828 into 1831). Causa criminal for the murder of two small children — María Antonia (~5 yrs) and Blas de Jesús (~1 yr), children of soldier Ignacio Olivas — and the estupro (rape) of the girl, on the denuncia of soldier Ignacio Ribas; accused: fellow SF-company soldier Francisco Rubis (25, native of Los Ángeles). Post-mortem autopsy 16 Aug 1828 by the English settler-physician Antonio [William A.] Richardson ("de nación inglés"): both children strangled with a cord, the girl raped. Evidence: parents at a fandango at Sra Bibiana's; a surviving small child blamed "un coyote muy bravo"; Rubis (who had quarreled with the mother 4 days before) tried that night to swap a clean shirt and passed off a bloodstained *sarape* (borrowed from Juan García, returned via young Rafael Pacheco) — central evidence; Rubis claimed the blood was sheep's (de borrego). Procedural wrangling over jurisdiction, fiscal turnover (Mariano G. Vallejo among fiscals), and the Monterey↔SF distance. Fiscal Padrés (27 May 1831) recommended sparing Rubis the death penalty given time served; ⭐⭐⭐ but Rubis was EXECUTED by garrote, maintaining his innocence — and per an inserted eyewitness reminiscence of Ignacio Cibrián (b. 18 Oct 1816, SF; he was ~14 at the execution), ~2 years later an Indian arrested for robbery/murder, sentenced to death, confessed that *he* had committed the crime and that "Rubis murió inocente" — a documented wrongful execution. (A Bancroft-collector's English note vouches: "Cibrián is a man of family… very honorable… living in the rancho of Salsi Puedes, Contra Costa County, near Mount Diablo.") An early CA forensic examination + a wrongful-execution case
