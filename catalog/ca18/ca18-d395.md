# ⭐⭐ Causa vs José Lucino (19), neófito of San José, for killing the foreigner "Rin" — an American, the blacksmith of San 

**C-A 18 (Dep. St. Pap. Benicia Mil., 1822–28), scan n447–n449** · tomo p. LXVIII.406–79 · 1829-04-25 · Savage abstract · ⭐⭐

⭐⭐ Causa vs José Lucino (19), neófito of San José, for killing the foreigner "Rin" — an American, the blacksmith of San José pueblo, who had gone out hunting beaver/otter (castores): Lucino lassoed him with his reata, dragged and killed him, stripped him, with accomplices Victorino & José Colosanes. Lucino's stated motive: he greeted the American "amar á Dios," and the American replied "Indianos mal," enraging him. The fiscal demands Lucino be shot (pasado por las armas) AND that 10 Indians from each mission be imprisoned as a warning ("escarmiento")
