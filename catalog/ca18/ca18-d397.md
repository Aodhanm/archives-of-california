# ⭐⭐ Actuaciones vs the captain & master of the Hawaiian (Sandwich Islands) brig-schooner "Karimoko" for touching at Santa

**C-A 18 (Dep. St. Pap. Benicia Mil., 1822–28), scan n450–n452** · tomo p. LXVIII.540–625 · 1828-11-28 · Savage abstract · ⭐⭐

⭐⭐ Actuaciones vs the captain & master of the Hawaiian (Sandwich Islands) brig-schooner "Karimoko" for touching at Santa Catalina Island, forming an establishment there, and smuggling goods into the rada de San Pedro (April 1828); Capt. "Juan Sola" deposed via interpreter — cargo from Hawaii (sugar, rice, tea, honey, tobacco, blankets); he had left 5 English sailors + 3 Hawaiian "canacas" (kanakas) + ~480 hides on Sta Catalina; Echeandía ordered Argüello to inventory the Catalina establishment; fine $300 for the illegal San Pedro trade + embargo, then released
