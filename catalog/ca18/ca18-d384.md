# ⭐⭐ Causa vs Serapio, neófito of Santa Clara, for the rape-murder of the boy Miguel (~11): Serapio confessed he forced th

**C-A 18 (Dep. St. Pap. Benicia Mil., 1822–28), scan n438–n439** · tomo p. LXVIII.527–37 · 1829-03-10 · Savage abstract · ⭐⭐

⭐⭐ Causa vs Serapio, neófito of Santa Clara, for the rape-murder of the boy Miguel (~11): Serapio confessed he forced the boy (who had refused), then stabbed and killed him to keep him silent (26 Dec 1827); while a fugitive he killed 10 Sta Clara mission cattle, took 9 horses, and took church asylum at Sta Clara; fiscal recommends expatriation from the province. ⚠ Includes a colonial-discourse marginal note: "Noticia de la sodomía entre los indios por el rumbo del Norte es algo común; lo mismo el estupro, y al matar las criaturas. Hay varios ejemplares públicos…" (a Spanish official generalizing about "the northern Indians" — fraught source for histories of sexuality/colonial discourse)
