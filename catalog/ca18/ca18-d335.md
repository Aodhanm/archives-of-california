# ⭐⭐ Sedition case vs Vicente Valencia, vecino of Sta Bárbara, for speaking in favor of the Spanish king against the Mexic

**C-A 18 (Dep. St. Pap. Benicia Mil., 1822–28), scan n317–n320** · tomo p. LXV.143–69 · 1830-02-15 (begun 1828-12-29) · Savage abstract · ⭐⭐

⭐⭐ Sedition case vs Vicente Valencia, vecino of Sta Bárbara, for speaking in favor of the Spanish king against the Mexican Republic: at a 12 Dec dance he reportedly said he had 22 yrs' arms service, "había salido con el Cristo á la espalda; pero que no se atenía á la Nación… se atenía al Rey," and that no resident of Sta Bárbara had sworn the federal Constitution or Mexican independence — declaring even the padres' oath (sworn under Capt. José de la Guerra / Lt. Maitorena) perjured; witnesses say he was drunk; defender Dip. Vicente Sánchez; after 14 months' prison Echeandía frees him (only drink, no real grounds)
