# ⭐⭐⭐ Investigation into the clandestine flight to Spain of Padre Fr. José Altimira (controversial founder of Mission San 

**C-A 18 (Dep. St. Pap. Benicia Mil., 1822–28), scan n426–n428+** · tomo p. LXVII.~189 ff · 1828-02-02 · Savage abstract · ⭐⭐⭐

⭐⭐⭐ Investigation into the clandestine flight to Spain of Padre Fr. José Altimira (controversial founder of Mission San Francisco Solano / Sonoma, 1823) aboard an English-captained ship, Jan 1828: depositions of escort cabo Juan Lugo and mayordomos Crisóstomo Lugo & Vicente Pico on the cargas of provisions, wine, soap, vinegar, chocolate — and possibly mission silver/money moved aboard via Sta Bárbara; Padre Francisco Suñer left holding only the keys to the money/clothing/bodega. Includes Altimira's own farewell letter to "Mi Sr Jorge [George] Coleman, inglés de nación y recién reconciliado por mí," written aboard a bergantín [ship name uncertain in MS] 23 Jan 1828, lamenting abandoning "esos pobres indios mis amados neófitos" and announcing his "larguísima caminada… por mar á España, mi patria"; letter closes "Tuyo = Fr. José Altimira" (ends n430, LXVII.215–227)
