# ⭐ Causa vs San Diego soldiers (Ramón Figueroa, Dámaso Alipaz, Trinidad, Martín Uriarte, Julián Blanco; 4 ringleaders pun

**C-A 18 (Dep. St. Pap. Benicia Mil., 1822–28), scan n390–n394** · tomo p. LXVI.393–441 · 1828-11-24 (begun 13 Oct) · Savage abstract · ⭐

⭐ Causa vs San Diego soldiers (Ramón Figueroa, Dámaso Alipaz, Trinidad, Martín Uriarte, Julián Blanco; 4 ringleaders punished: Blas Olivas, Miguel Alvarado, Ignacio Alipaz, Julián Blanco) for collectively petitioning their commander, Tte Cnl Santiago Argüello, for their back pay (alcances) — a near-mutiny; Argüello allegedly retorted that mission Indians would serve "con más sumisión y obediencia" and the soldiers weren't needed; the soldiers pleaded they "no podían hacer el servicio por hallarse desnudos y descalzos" (naked & barefoot, ashamed to appear); punishment: the 4 ringleaders dispersed to the Monterey / S. Francisco / Sta Bárbara companies. Savage's note: "La cuerda siempre se rompe por lo más delgado"
